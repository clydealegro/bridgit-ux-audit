A/B Test Priority Queue

10 priority tests · 6-week sequence · 23 backlog hypotheses Ordered by impact × ease
🧪
How to read this queue Tests are ranked by expected impact (traffic volume × estimated conversion lift) weighted against implementation effort. All 10 tests require no new form logic or back-end integration. Tests 1–9 require only copy edits, link additions, or minor layout changes. Run all tests at a 50/50 split unless noted. Minimum detectable effect: 5% relative uplift. Recommended sample size: 1,000 sessions per variant before reading results.

Scenario-specific CTA copy vs generic "Apply now"
F-NAV-01 All 7 solution pages Low effort High priority
Current state
Every solution page uses "Apply now" linking to apply.bridgit.com.au — identical across all 7 scenarios and all 8 personas.
Proposed change
Replace with scenario-specific labels:
Upsize → "Start my upsize application" • Downsize → "Check if I'm eligible to downsize" • Renovate → "Start my renovation bridge" • Retire → "Speak to a retirement specialist" • Separate → "Get a confidential assessment" • Invest → "Get investor pricing" • House & Land → "Start my house & land bridge"
Hypothesis
Changing the primary CTA from "Apply now" to a scenario-specific label will increase CTA click-through rate across all solution pages because users feel confirmed they are in the right place for their specific situation, reducing the hesitation that comes from generic transactional language on high-consideration financial pages.
Primary metric
CTA click-through rate (solution page → apply.bridgit.com.au)
Secondary metric
Time on page; scroll depth per solution page
Effort
Low — copy change only, no dev required
Personas
P01 P02 P03 P04 P05 P06 P07 P08
Test type
A/B 50/50 — run independently per solution page; aggregate for overall signal

"Check my eligibility" secondary CTA on Calculator result
F-NAV-05/10 Calculator result Low effort High-intent
Current state
Post-calculation CTA is "Get a quote" linking directly to apply.bridgit.com.au/customer-quote. There is no path from calculator result to the Eligibility checker, breaking journey J1 (P01: Calculator → Eligibility → Apply).
Proposed change
Add "Check my eligibility first" as a secondary CTA below "Get a quote" in the calculator result state. Control: "Get a quote" only. Variant: "Get a quote" + "Check my eligibility".
Hypothesis
Adding "Check my eligibility" as a secondary CTA on calculator results will increase Eligibility page visits from Calculator and downstream apply rate, because users who self-qualify through the Eligibility checker convert at a higher rate than those pushed directly to a quote form before they have confirmed their own readiness.
Primary metric
Eligibility page visits attributed to Calculator; Apply conversion rate (calculator entrants)
Secondary metric
Funnel completion rate: Calculator → Eligibility → Apply (J1 journey)
Effort
Low — single secondary CTA addition in calculator widget

Email CTA alongside phone for async-preferring users
F-NAV-04 Homepage, Bridging Loan, Eligibility Medium effort Persona-specific
Current state
All contact routes through phone (tel:1300141161) or "Connect with an expert" (schedule-a-call). Zero mailto: links across all 13 pages confirmed. P08 and P06 cannot initiate contact outside business hours without committing to a callback.
Proposed change
Add a visible email CTA (mailto:hello@bridgit.com.au or async inquiry form) alongside the phone number on Homepage contact block, Bridging Loan page, and Eligibility page bottom. Control: phone + schedule-a-call only. Variant: phone + schedule-a-call + email CTA.
Hypothesis
Surfacing an async email contact option alongside phone will increase total contact conversion rate for Regional Relocators (P08) and time-poor Investors (P06) because it removes the barrier of committing to a synchronous phone call, recovering abandonment from users who research outside business hours or in low-connectivity regional environments.
Primary metric
Contact conversion rate — schedule-a-call + email combined vs schedule-a-call alone
Secondary metric
Contacts initiated outside 9am–5:30pm AEDT (proxy for async preference); contact channel split
Effort
Medium — design + mailto link or simple inquiry form integration

F-HP-01 Homepage hero Low effort First-touch
Current state

LVR plain language + worked cost example on Rates & Fees
F-RF-02 Rates & Fees Medium effort Consideration-stage
Current state
Rates & Fees page presents LVR and fee data in technical format without plain-language definitions or a worked cost example. P03 (Renovator) and P06 (Investor) must manually calculate total loan cost, introducing decision friction at the consideration stage.
Proposed change
Add: (1) LVR plain-language tooltip/inline definition ("LVR = loan amount ÷ property value. At 60% LVR on a $600K property, you can borrow $360K.") and (2) a worked cost example showing a representative scenario (e.g. $300K bridge, 6 months, 7.5% p.a., total cost breakdown). Control: current page. Variant: page + LVR definition + worked example.
Hypothesis
Adding LVR plain-language explanation and a worked cost example to Rates & Fees will increase apply rate from cost-researching personas, because reducing the mental effort required to calculate total loan cost removes the primary decision barrier for research-driven and cost-comparison-focused borrowers at the consideration stage.
Primary metric
Apply rate from Rates & Fees page visitors; CTA click-through from R&F page
Secondary metric
Time-on-page; scroll depth; exit rate from Rates & Fees
Effort
Medium — content design + inline tooltip or expandable definition component

F-SOL-ALL-01 All 7 solution pages High effort 7-page reach
Current state
All 7 solution pages display the same generic Trustpilot testimonial carousel, not filtered or matched by scenario. A P01 Upsizer on the Upsize page may read a testimonial from a Downsizer. No scenario or persona matching exists.
Proposed change

Apply rate per solution page; testimonial section engagement (scroll, clicks)

Separation FAQ + explicit entry on Eligibility page
F-ELG-02 Eligibility Low effort Anxiety persona
Current state
The Eligibility page uses generic criteria lists with no specific FAQ entry for borrowers going through separation. P04 (Separating Homeowner) cannot confirm whether they are eligible without reading through full criteria that were not written for their scenario.
Proposed change
Add a Separation scenario FAQ entry: "Am I eligible if I'm going through a separation? Yes — if the property is in your name or jointly owned, you can still apply. We handle separation scenarios discreetly." Alongside this, add an explicit entry point ("Separating? Start here") linking to the Separate solution page. Control: current generic criteria. Variant: criteria + separation FAQ + explicit CTA.
Hypothesis
Adding a separation-specific FAQ entry and explicit entry point to the Eligibility page will increase "Connect with an expert" CTA click-through rate from P04 visitors, because explicitly confirming eligibility for their specific scenario reduces anxiety and removes the primary barrier to engagement for the highest-anxiety persona in the audit.
Primary metric
"Connect with an expert" CTA CTR from Eligibility page (P04 proxy: visitors from Separate page or separation-related search terms)
Secondary metric
Eligibility page exit rate; Separate page visits from Eligibility
Effort
Low — FAQ copy addition and single CTA link to Separate page
