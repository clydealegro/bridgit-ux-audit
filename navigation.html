All 10 Findings

Click any finding card to expand the full detail. Ordered by severity.

High Severity (4)
F-NAV-01
Generic "Apply now" CTA on all 7 solution pages — not scenario-specific
▼
High Low effort Quick win All personas All solution pages
Finding
Every solution page — Upsize, Downsize, Renovate, Retire, Separate, Invest, House & Land — uses the same primary CTA: "Apply now" linking to apply.bridgit.com.au. No page-specific or persona-specific CTA copy exists on any of the 7 pages.
Evidence
HTML across all solution pages: <a href="https://apply.bridgit.com.au/..." class="button is-nav w-button">Apply now</a>
Impact
P04 Separating Homeowner reads "Apply now" on a separation-focused page — feels clinical, not empathetic. P07 Mortgage Broker sees a consumer CTA on a broker page. P03 and P05 have complex scenarios where a specific label confirms they are in the right place.
Personas affected
All (P01–P08) — disproportionate impact on P04, P07
Recommendation
Rewrite CTA copy per page: Upsize → "Start my upsize application" • Downsize → "Check if I'm eligible to downsize" • Renovate → "Start my renovation bridge" • Retire → "Speak to a retirement specialist" • Separate → "Get a confidential assessment" • Invest → "Get investor pricing" • House & Land → "Start my house & land bridge"
A/B hypothesis
Scenario-specific CTA labels will increase click-through rate vs generic "Apply now" because users feel confirmed in their specific scenario and intent. See A/B Test #1.
F-NAV-03
Rates & Fees buried in secondary nav tier — no in-body link from Homepage or most solution pages
▼
High Medium effort P01 P02 P03 P06 P08 Homepage, Solution pages
Finding
Rates & Fees (/rates-and-fees) is accessible via a two-click path — Loans → About our loans → Rates — but has no in-body link from the Homepage. In-body links confirmed on Downsize, House & Land, Invest, Renovate; unconfirmed for Upsize, Retire, Separate.
Nav structure
Loans (dropdown) → About our loans (group label) → Rates (/rates-and-fees). The "About our loans" group label acts as a visual blocker before users see the Rates link.
Impact
Every borrower needs cost/rate information before converting. Burying this page may create a trust gap — users may assume fees are hidden or unclear. Affects P01 (research-driven), P02 (plain-language cost summary needed), P03 (cost-comparison-focused), P06 (total cost of capital calculation).
Recommendation
Add a prominent in-body "Rates & Fees" link/card on the Homepage and all solution pages, particularly at the consideration-stage scroll position.
F-NAV-04
No async email contact option anywhere on site — P08 and P06 cannot reach out without calling
▼
High Medium effort P08 P06 Site-wide
Finding
All contact routes through phone (tel:1300141161) or /schedule-a-call ("Connect with an expert"). Zero mailto: links found across all 13 pages. Both contact paths require a synchronous commitment — either a live call or a scheduled callback.
Evidence
Grep across all HTML files for mailto: returned zero results.
Impact
P08 Regional Relocator: prefers async communication; cannot email; must call (business hours only 9am–5:30pm AEDT) or schedule a callback. P06 Property Investor: time-poor; email inquiry fits workflow better than a scheduled call. Both personas may abandon entirely.
Recommendation
Add mailto:hello@bridgit.com.au as a visible contact option on Eligibility, Calculator result, and Contact pages. Consider adding an async inquiry form (name, phone, question) as a lower-commitment alternative to a call.
A/B hypothesis
Adding an email CTA alongside "Connect with an expert" will increase contact conversion rate for async-preferring visitors. See A/B Test #3 and A/B Test #10.
F-NAV-05 / F-NAV-10
Calculator result CTA bypasses Eligibility checker — no direct path from Calculator to Eligibility (J1 journey broken)
▼
High Low effort Quick win P01 P03 P05 P08 Calculator → Eligibility
Finding
Post-result CTA is "Get a quote" linking directly to apply.bridgit.com.au/customer-quote. No secondary CTA to check eligibility. A hidden /schedule-a-call link exists in the calculator widget (display: none) but is not visible. This breaks journey J1: Homepage → Calculator → Eligibility → Apply.
Evidence
Calculator widget HTML: <a href="https://apply.bridgit.com.au/customer-quote?..."><span>Get a quote</span></a>
Impact
Users who calculated their loan cost but are uncertain about eligibility are presented "Get a quote" (implies ready to apply) rather than "Check my eligibility" (confirms qualification first). Creates premature application drop-off for uncertain users and abandonment for those not ready to quote.
Recommendation
Add "Check my eligibility" as a secondary CTA below "Get a quote" in the calculator result state. Alternatively, restructure as a linear funnel: Calculator → Eligibility → Apply with CTAs at each step.
A/B hypothesis
Adding "Check my eligibility" as a secondary CTA will increase Eligibility page visits and downstream apply rate because users who self-qualify convert at a higher rate. See A/B Test #2.
F-NAV-06
Separate page H1 frames separation as a finance problem — misses emotional acknowledgment first
▼
High Low effort Quick win P04 Solution: Separate
Current H1
"Move on without finance holding you back" — frames the situation as a finance problem before acknowledging the life event.
Schema description (not on page)
"A clean break should have a clear path. Bridgit lets you access the equity in your home before you sell, so you can get to what's next — cleanly, quickly and with clarity." More empathetic, but only visible to search engines.
Impact
P04 (Separating Homeowner) is emotionally stressed and time-pressured. They fear clinical financial language and intrusive questions. A transactional headline on a high-anxiety page may increase bounce rate and reduce "Connect with an expert" engagement.
Recommendation
Rewrite H1 to lead with empathy: "When life changes, your next step shouldn't have to wait." Then subhead explains the financial solution. Move the privacy/discretion signal higher on the page above the fold.
A/B hypothesis
Empathy-led H1 will increase scroll depth and "Connect with an expert" click rate from P04 vs. transactional framing because P04 needs to feel understood before engaging with financial details. See A/B Test #5.
Medium Severity (4)
F-NAV-02
No visual breadcrumbs on solution pages — breadcrumb data exists in JSON-LD schema only
▼
Medium Low effort Quick win All personas All solution pages
Finding
All solution and detail pages have breadcrumb data in JSON-LD structured data markup (e.g. Upsize: Home > Bridging > Upsizing), but no visual breadcrumb trail is rendered in the page UI. Users cannot see where they are in the site hierarchy.
Evidence
JSON-LD on upsize.html: @type: BreadcrumbList with items at positions 1 (Home /), 2 (Bridging /bridging), 3 (Upsizing /bridging/upsizing). Note: schema paths use /bridging/upsizing, not current URL structure /bridging-loan/upsize — potential schema staleness.
Impact
Users arriving via Google direct to a solution page cannot see site hierarchy or navigate back to the parent Bridging Loan page without using browser back. Higher concern for P02 Downsizing Empty Nester (less digitally confident).
Recommendation
Implement visible breadcrumbs matching actual URL structure (Home > Bridging Loan > [Solution]). Ensure schema and visual breadcrumbs are consistent. Resolve schema path mismatch.
F-NAV-07
Solutions dropdown category labels add cognitive load for first-time users
▼
Medium Low effort P07 P01 Global nav
Finding
The Solutions dropdown uses category group labels (e.g. "About our loans") that add a scanning step before users see the actual page links. First-time users who are unfamiliar with the site structure may experience hesitation or miss items.
Impact
Low-to-medium. Most returning visitors and referred users (P07 Broker) know the nav structure. Most at risk: cold-discovery users encountering the dropdown for the first time on mobile, where the category label takes up vertical space.
Recommendation
Consider flattening the Solutions dropdown on mobile or A/B testing flat list vs. categorised sub-menu to measure discovery improvement. See F-NAV-M07 in Phase 4.
F-NAV-09
No cross-linking between related/adjacent solution pages
▼
Medium Medium effort P02 P04 P03 P05 All solution pages
Finding
Solution pages do not link to related/adjacent solutions within their body content. Missing links: Downsize ↔ Retire (same persona P02, different scenario); Separate → Invest (P04 may need to purchase post-separation); Renovate → Upsize (renovation may precede upsize).
Impact
P02 Downsizing Empty Nester may land on Downsize but their real situation is a retirement-driven downsize — covered more specifically by Retire. Without a contextual cross-link, they may accept a slightly-off framing or miss the most relevant page entirely.
Recommendation
Add a "Related scenarios" section at the bottom of each solution page linking to 1–2 adjacent solutions. Examples: Downsize page → "Also exploring retirement? See our Retire solution." Renovate page → "Planning to upsize after renovating? See Upsize."
J3 Gap
No in-body link from Bridging Loan page to Rates & Fees — breaks J3 due diligence flow
▼
Medium Medium effort P03 P06 P01 Bridging Loan
Finding
The Bridging Loan page does not have a confirmed in-body link to Rates & Fees. P03's natural J3 journey (Bridging Loan → Rates & Fees → Eligibility → Apply) requires returning to the nav dropdown after each page — three separate dropdown interactions within one research session.
Impact
Research-driven personas (P03 Renovator, P06 Investor, P01 Upsizer) follow a structured due-diligence path. Without in-body contextual links, each step requires deliberate nav exploration, reducing research velocity and increasing drop-off at each transition.
Recommendation
Add an in-body "Rates & Fees" card or link in the Bridging Loan page body, ideally at the consideration-stage scroll position after the "How it works" steps. This mirrors the pattern already confirmed on Downsize, H&L, Invest, and Renovate pages.
Low Severity (2)
F-NAV-07
"Partners" nav label may not self-identify for cold-discovery brokers
▼
Low Low effort P07 Global nav
Finding
Main nav label is "Partners" — not "Brokers" or "For Brokers." A broker discovering Bridgit for the first time without prior marketing exposure may not immediately self-identify with "Partners." The sub-label "Become a partner" clarifies intent, but only after the dropdown is opened.
Impact
Low. Most brokers arrive via word-of-mouth or Bridgit BDM outreach and will know to look for "Partners." Minor acquisition friction risk for cold-discovery visits only.
Recommendation
A/B test "Brokers" vs "Partners" as the nav label. Alternatively, add a hover subtitle "Mortgage brokers & referrers" visible on dropdown open.
F-NAV-08
"Log in" in primary nav alongside "Apply now" creates ambiguity for new prospects
▼
Low Low effort P01 P02 P03 P04 Global nav
Finding
Header nav displays: [Phone] [Apply now] [Log in]. "Log in" sits as a secondary CTA alongside "Apply now" for all visitors including first-time prospects who have no existing account.
Impact
Low. May cause minor hesitation ("do I need to log in to apply?"). Not a strong friction point but adds unnecessary cognitive burden for new visitors who have no account and no reason to log in.
Recommendation
Demote "Log in" to a utility position (above the main nav or inside a dropdown). Add microcopy near "Apply now" clarifying no account is needed to start an application.
