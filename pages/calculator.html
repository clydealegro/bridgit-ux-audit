F-CALC-01 No "how to use" guidance; blank-slate inputs with no defaults ▼
Low Quick Win Effort: Low P01 — Upsizer P03 — Renovator P05 — Builder P08 — Regional
Finding
The calculator has no "how to use this calculator" help section. Input field default values are unknown — if fields start empty, users face blank-slate paralysis (no sense of what a typical loan looks like). For P08 (Regional Relocator researching async) and P05 (House & Land Builder with an unusual scenario), a pre-populated example ("e.g., $850,000 current home value") would reduce hesitation.
Recommendation
Pre-populate fields with representative default values (median Australian property price scenarios). Add a brief "How to use this calculator" tooltip or collapsed section.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases calculator → apply completion rate by surfacing next-step guidance post-calculation.
F-CALC-M02 "LVR" displayed in results modal without plain-language definition ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low P08 — Regional P02 — Downsizer
Finding
The results modal shows "Your estimated principal is $7,494,000 (LVR 78%)" — "LVR" is displayed without a tooltip or inline definition. P08 (Regional Relocator, moderate financial literacy) and P02 (Empty Nester, non-financial background) are likely calculator users who will not know that LVR means Loan-to-Value Ratio. At the moment of highest intent (just calculated their loan), unexplained jargon introduces doubt and may trigger abandonment before tapping "Get a quote".
Recommendation
Change "(LVR 78%)" to "(Loan-to-Value Ratio: 78%)" inline, or add a tappable ⓘ icon next to the LVR figure that shows a one-line tooltip: "LVR = your loan as a % of the property value".
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — reduces confusion in results modal by defining LVR in plain language at point of first use.
F-CALC-M03 "Refinance existing mortgage" label creates bridging loan confusion in results summary ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low All Personas
Finding
The results modal summary table shows line items: "Purchase price / Refinance existing mortgage / Less your contribution / Total". The label "Refinance existing mortgage" is misleading in a bridging loan context — Bridgit is not refinancing the mortgage; the bridging loan sits alongside the existing mortgage until the current home sells. Users who read "refinance" may believe they need to refinance their home loan to qualify, which is incorrect and may cause pre-qualified users to self-exclude.
Recommendation
Rename the label to "Existing mortgage on property being sold" or "Current mortgage (to be settled at sale)" to accurately describe what the figure represents in a bridging loan scenario.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — reduces mortgage/bridge confusion in results summary by using bridging-specific label.
