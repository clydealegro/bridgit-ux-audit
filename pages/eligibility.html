F-ELG-01 No self-serve eligibility checker; page is purely informational ▼
High Strategic Effort: High All Personas
Finding
The Eligibility page is a static informational page listing criteria. There is no interactive "Do I qualify?" form or self-serve checker. Users who are uncertain about their eligibility must read all criteria, make their own assessment, and either proceed to Apply or call. This creates friction for every persona — particularly P02 (Downsizer, lower digital confidence) and P08 (Regional Relocator, prefers async).
Recommendation
Build a 4–6 question eligibility pre-screener with conditional logic that gives a directional answer ("Based on what you've told us, you're likely eligible. Here's your next step."). This should capture a lead even on uncertain outcomes. This is a strategic investment but one of the highest-converting additions available for a regulated lending product.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases qualified apply rate by filtering out ineligible visitors before form submission.
F-ELG-02 Separation/divorce scenario absent from eligibility criteria ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Low P04 — Separating
Finding
The Eligibility page lists PAYG, self-employed, retirees, and company/trust applicants but does not address the separation scenario. P04 (Separating Homeowner) has unique eligibility questions: Can I apply if my income has changed due to separation? Can I apply if my partner is still on the mortgage? Will the settlement process affect the application? This absence could cause P04 to self-exclude and not apply.
Recommendation
Add an FAQ or eligibility note addressing separation: "Going through separation? You can still access your equity. Call us or connect with an expert to understand your options — we handle this sensitively."
Impact of Change
Consideration → CTA — increases expert CTA clicks from highest-anxiety separation persona by validating their scenario.
A/B Hypothesis
Adding separation-specific eligibility guidance to the Eligibility page will increase "Connect with an expert" clicks from users in the separation scenario because P04 currently has no signal that they qualify. Test: Eligibility page with vs. without separation FAQ entry. Primary metric: "Connect with an expert" click-through rate on Eligibility page. Secondary metric: Applications sourced from Separate solution page.
F-ELG-03 No plain-language summary before formal criteria ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low All Personas
Finding
The reassurance opening ("every situation is unique") is good, but the page then moves directly into formal eligibility criteria without a plain-language summary of who typically qualifies. For P02 (lower digital confidence) and P08 (prefers async self-service), a 4-bullet quick summary ("You'll likely qualify if: you own Australian property, you're 18+, you have a good credit history, your property is residential") would reduce cognitive load before the detail.
Recommendation
Add a "Quick answer" or "Who typically qualifies" summary section before the detailed criteria list.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — reduces pre-application drop-off by clarifying criteria legibility.
F-ELG-04 Self-employed criteria use accounting jargon (ABN, YTD, BAS) without definitions ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low P03 — Renovator P06 — Investor
Finding
The self-employed eligibility requirements reference ABN, YTD income, and BAS without defining these terms. While P03 (Equity-Unlock Renovator, Architect/SBO) and P06 (Property Investor) likely know these terms, they are jargon for anyone else who is self-employed without accounting experience.
Recommendation
Add inline plain-language clarifications: "ABN (Australian Business Number)", "YTD income (your earnings so far this financial year)", "BAS (Business Activity Statement, your regular ATO reporting)". These can be tooltips to avoid cluttering the layout.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — reduces abandonment for non-standard borrowers by explicitly addressing renovation and investment scenarios.
F-ELG-05 Regional property exclusions are vague ("some regional areas excluded") ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low P08 — Regional
Finding
The Eligibility page states "most metro and non-metro locations (some regional areas excluded)" without specifying which regions are excluded. For P08 (Regional Relocator), this vagueness creates anxiety — they cannot self-serve to confirm their area is covered and must contact Bridgit to find out. This is a friction point that may cause abandonment for the regional persona.
Recommendation
Either list the specific exclusions (e.g., "excludes properties in remote/rural areas below X population threshold") or reframe as "available in all major metro and regional centres — contact us if your area is not listed."
Impact of Change
Consideration → CTA — recovers interstate/interstate-adjacent P08 abandonment by confirming geographic eligibility.
F-ELG-M01 No email contact path blocks P08 from async eligibility check ▼
Critical Quick Win Effort: Low P08 — Regional
Finding
Eligibility page is the primary destination for P08 (Regional Relocator) to verify whether their property location and situation qualifies. The page has only tel: contact options. P08's explicit digital behaviour is email-over-phone, and they may be in a regional area with patchy voice connectivity. Without an email CTA, P08's only next step is a phone call — their least preferred action — or application submission without having confirmed eligibility. Either outcome leads to abandonment or unqualified applications. Cross-reference: F-NAV-04 (Phase 2), F-ELG-05 (Phase 3).
Recommendation
Add a prominent email CTA at the bottom of the eligibility criteria section: "Not sure if you qualify? Email us — hello@bridgit.com.au". Consider a pre-filled subject line: ?subject=Eligibility%20question.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Contact — removes P08's only barrier to async eligibility check; critical mobile conversion path.
A/B Hypothesis
Adding an email CTA on the Eligibility page will directly increase contact rate from P08 and reduce page abandonment without phone commitment.
F-ELG-M02 Accordion toggles may have insufficient tap target size for P02 ▼
Medium Medium Effort Effort: Low P02 — Downsizer P08 — Regional
Finding
Eligibility accordions stack correctly on mobile but accordion toggle areas appear to have minimal padding from screenshot inspection. P02 (62F, Downsizer) may use Eligibility to check their retiree eligibility, and older users require larger touch targets for accurate tapping. WCAG 2.5.5 recommends 44×44px minimum; Apple HIG recommends 44px.
Recommendation
Ensure accordion toggle buttons have min-height: 48px and padding: 12px 16px minimum. Test on a 390px device for accurate tap registration.
Impact of Change
Usability — reduces mis-taps on accordion toggles for older and motor-impaired users.
