Page Audit

Homepage

Personas: All (first touch) 5 desktop findings · 1 mobile finding
● 2 High ● 3 Medium
📏 Scroll Depth Impact
Analytics confirm poor scroll depth site-wide. On the homepage — the highest-traffic entry point — most users exit before reaching the content most likely to convert them. This is not a content quality problem; it is a content hierarchy problem. The sections that build trust and drive action are buried below the depth threshold where most visitors stop scrolling. See Pattern P4 in the Executive Summary for the cross-site view.
Page Section Approx. Depth Conversion-Critical Element Status
Hero 0–15% Trust signals (ACL, Trustpilot, AFCA) ❌ Absent above fold
How it works 15–35% Process clarity, timeline ✅ Visible
Solution scenarios 35–55% Persona routing — "find your scenario" ⚠️ Generic, no routing
Social proof / testimonials 55–70% Credibility for undecided visitors ❌ Below typical exit point
Eligibility / primary CTA 70–85% Conversion entry point for qualified visitors ❌ Most users never reach
Core hierarchy problem: The two elements users need most before converting — trust signals and an eligibility entry point — are placed at the bottom of the page where analytics show most visitors have already exited. Moving trust signals into the hero and adding a mid-page eligibility CTA at ~40% depth would intercept the majority of users before they leave. This is a layout reorder, not a redesign.
Related findings: F-HP-CRIT-01 · F-HP-02 · F-SOL-ALL-01 · See Pattern P4 in Executive Summary
F-HP-01 ACL number visible in footer only, not above the fold ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Low All Personas
Finding
ACL 532542 is present only in the footer ("Australian Credit Licence Number 532542, ABN 16 644 588 161"). For a high-consideration financial product in a regulated category, relegating the licence number to the footer reduces immediate trust credibility — especially for first-time visitors who land mid-page via paid search.
Recommendation
Add ACL 532542 to the hero section or the trust signal bar (alongside Trustpilot), with a link to the ASIC register. A small badge or one-line format is sufficient: "ACL 532542 | Regulated by ASIC".
Impact of Change
Regulatory trust — reduces ACL-related abandonment at first touch for all personas.
A/B Hypothesis
Adding ACL 532542 as a visible trust badge in the hero section will increase hero-to-CTA click rate because first-time visitors to a financial product site need to see regulatory legitimacy before engaging. Test: With ACL badge in hero vs. footer-only. Primary metric: Hero "Apply now" + "Connect with an expert" click-through rate. Secondary metric: Time on page, bounce rate.
F-HP-02 Testimonials are not scenario-labelled; personas cannot self-identify ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Low All Personas
Finding
The homepage Trustpilot/testimonial carousel exists and shows 5-star rated reviews. However, testimonials are not labelled with persona context (e.g., "Sarah — Upsizer, Sydney" or "Michael — renovated before selling"). A Confident Upsizer reading a review from a retiree downsizer cannot identify with the outcome. Generic testimonials are weaker social proof than scenario-matched ones.
Recommendation
Label each testimonial with the customer's scenario/use case. Source and display at least one testimonial per major scenario (upsize, downsize, renovate, separate) on the homepage carousel.
Impact of Change
Consideration → CTA — increases hero CTA click-through by substantiating credibility at first scroll.
A/B Hypothesis
Scenario-labelled testimonials ("I was upsizing and...") will increase engagement with the testimonial section and downstream CTA click rate vs. generic testimonials because P01/P02/P03 each need to see someone like themselves succeeding. Test: Generic carousel vs. scenario-labelled carousel. Primary metric: CTA click-through rate from homepage. Secondary metric: Testimonial carousel engagement (swipe/scroll depth).
F-HP-03 No macro credibility statistics visible on page ▼
Medium Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
No volume or scale statistics are visible on the homepage (e.g., "17,000+ accredited brokers", "Funded $XM+ in bridging loans", "X,000 homeowners helped"). Fintechs with traction use these numbers to shortcut trust. Bridgit has the assets (17,000+ broker network is cited in the Persona Library) but they do not appear to be surfaced on the homepage.
Recommendation
Add a stat bar or social proof section with 2–3 credibility statistics. Suggested candidates: broker network size, number of borrowers helped, average approval time, total funded.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases return visits and direct apply rate by reducing broker dependency.
F-HP-04 No embedded eligibility entry point in the homepage body ▼
Medium Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
There is no embedded eligibility checker or "quick check" widget on the homepage. Users who want to self-qualify must navigate to a separate Eligibility page. For a product where the primary anxiety is "do I even qualify?", removing this step from the homepage increases drop-off before intent is formed.
Recommendation
Embed a 3-question eligibility pre-screener directly on the homepage (property value, mortgage balance, property type) that provides an instant directional answer and captures a lead.
Impact of Change
CTA conversion — increases Apply CTA clicks by removing vague "Find out more" friction.
F-HP-05 No privacy assurance near conversion CTAs ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low All Personas
Finding
The "Apply now" and "Connect with an expert" CTAs have no privacy assurance copy adjacent to them (e.g., "No credit check. Your data is safe." or a privacy policy link). For a financial product, particularly for anxiety-prone personas (P04 Separating Homeowner, P02 Downsizer), assurance near the CTA reduces pre-click hesitation.
Recommendation
Add 1-line microcopy beneath each primary CTA: "No credit check to get started. Privacy Policy." with link.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases self-service apply rate by signalling process transparency.
F-HP-M01 No email / async contact path on Homepage ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Low P08 — Regional
Finding
Homepage provides only tel: links for contact. No mailto: link exists anywhere on the page. P08 (Regional Relocator, async-preferring, often in low-connectivity areas) cannot initiate contact via email. On mobile, in a regional setting with poor signal, a phone call may not be possible. This is a complete contact path gap for P08's primary communication preference. Cross-reference: F-NAV-04 (Phase 2).
Recommendation
Add mailto:hello@bridgit.com.au as a contact option in the footer or contact section, clearly labelled "Email us". Consider a secondary CTA: "Prefer to email? hello@bridgit.com.au" near the phone number on mobile.
Impact of Change
Lead generation — recovers async P08 abandonment on mobile by removing phone-call dependency.
A/B Hypothesis
Adding a visible email CTA alongside the phone number will increase contact initiation from async-preferring P08 and regional users, recovering abandonment from mobile users who cannot or will not call.
