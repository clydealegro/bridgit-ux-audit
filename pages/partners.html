F-PTR-01 Social proof is customer testimonials, not broker testimonials ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium P07 — Broker
Finding
The testimonials on the Partners page are from end customers (e.g., "The process was so quick and easy… I managed to sign my declaration between the 10th and 11th hole at my golf course" — Kay, Bridgit customer). Customer testimonials are the wrong social proof for a broker audience. Brokers need to see peer validation: other brokers praising Bridgit's speed, BDM responsiveness, and client outcomes.
Recommendation
Source 2–3 broker-specific testimonials (e.g., "As a broker, I refer bridging cases to Bridgit because their 24-hour approval means my clients don't miss out. And their BDMs actually pick up the phone."). Place these above the customer testimonials or as the primary social proof section.
Impact of Change
Partner → Referral conversion — increases broker referral volume by demonstrating active lender-broker partnership.
F-PTR-02 BDM locator requires a separate page visit; not integrated on Partners page ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium P07 — Broker
Finding
"Find your BDM" is a separate page (/find-your-bdm) accessible via the Partners nav dropdown. The Partners page itself does not appear to include an integrated BDM locator. For P07 (Mortgage Broker), who values low-friction access to a named BDM contact, requiring a separate page visit adds unnecessary steps to what should be the first interaction.
Recommendation
Embed a BDM finder card section directly on the Partners page — state selector → BDM name, photo, phone number, email. This mirrors what high-converting broker-facing lender pages do and immediately demonstrates Bridgit's commitment to broker relationships.
Impact of Change
Partner → Submit — increases broker deal submissions by removing form friction and adding BDM direct contact.
F-PTR-03 H1 "Partner with Bridgit" is functional, not benefit-led ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low P07 — Broker
Finding
"Partner with Bridgit" tells brokers what Bridgit wants (a partnership) rather than what brokers get. The schema description contains the genuine broker value: "24-hour approval, no clawbacks, broker fees paid upfront, trail available on 24-month loan terms." None of these differentiated benefits appear in the H1 or hero subhead.
Recommendation
Rewrite H1 to lead with broker benefit: "Give your clients 24-hour bridging approvals. No clawbacks. Fees paid upfront." — or use a benefit-led subheadline if the brand voice requires a softer H1.
Impact of Change
Partner → Submit — reduces broker drop-off by clearly labelling submission path.
F-PTR-M01 BDM contact card has no email path; phone-only friction for brokers ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Low P07 — Broker
Finding
The .bdm_card-information component (confirmed in HTML, line 835) has a tel: link for phone contact but no mailto: for email. P07 (Mortgage Broker, efficiency-focused) may prefer to send an initial email query — especially when calling is not practical during client meetings. No email option means BDM contact is phone-only, increasing friction for the channel most critical to broker acquisition volume.
Recommendation
Add a mailto: link to each BDM card alongside the phone number (e.g., partnerships@bridgit.com.au or BDM-specific email). Label it "Email [BDM Name]" with a 44px tap target.
Impact of Change
Partner → Contact — increases broker async inquiries by providing email path alongside phone CTA.
A/B Hypothesis
Adding email contact alongside phone in BDM cards will increase broker inquiry rate from mobile users who are in client meetings or prefer async communication.
F-PTR-M02 BDM card may compress on small screens reducing tap accuracy ▼
Medium Medium Effort Effort: Low P07 — Broker
Finding
The BDM card uses flex: 1 layout. On 390px, flex items may stack vertically with tight spacing between BDM name, title, and phone number, making the phone number tap target less accurate. Mis-taps on a phone number that dial the wrong BDM create a poor first impression with a channel-critical persona.
Recommendation
Ensure BDM card on mobile has min-height: 120px; phone number element has min-height: 44px and adequate line-height (at least 1.5rem). Test card layout at 390px.
Impact of Change
Usability — reduces mis-taps on BDM contact card for mobile brokers.
