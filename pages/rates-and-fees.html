F-RF-01 Comparison rate disclosure not confirmed; potential ACL compliance gap ▼
Critical Quick Win Effort: Low All Personas
Finding
The comparison rate / AAPR disclosure was not found in structured data or meta content for the Rates & Fees page. Under the National Consumer Credit Protection Act (NCCP), a comparison rate must be displayed alongside advertised interest rates for consumer credit products. If this disclosure is absent from the page, it is an ACL compliance risk (ACL 532542). Note: the disclosure may exist in the page HTML body not captured by schema extraction — this finding requires visual verification in Phase 4.
Recommendation
Immediately verify that a comparison rate is present on the Rates & Fees page and that it follows NCCP formatting requirements (correct calculation basis, warning text). If absent, add it as an urgent fix before any other CRO work.
Impact of Change
Regulatory trust — eliminates compliance risk and prevents all-persona abandonment triggered by missing comparison rate disclosure.
F-RF-02 LVR not explained in plain language ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Low All Personas
Finding
The meta description references "up to 85% LVR" without a plain-language definition. For P02 (Downsizing Empty Nester) and P04 (Separating Homeowner) — who are less financially sophisticated — LVR is jargon. A user who doesn't know their LVR cannot determine if they qualify or how much they can borrow.
Recommendation
Add a plain-language explanation wherever LVR first appears: "LVR (Loan-to-Value Ratio) — this means you can borrow up to 85% of your property's market value." Consider a tooltip or callout box.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases apply rate from cost-researching personas by making total cost scannable.
F-RF-03 No worked cost example with real dollar amounts ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
The Rates & Fees page lists percentage rates and dollar fees but provides no worked example showing total cost for a typical scenario. Analytical personas (P01 Upsizer, P03 Renovator, P06 Investor) want to understand the total cost of borrowing — not just isolated line items. Without an example, users cannot self-assess value vs. alternatives (refinancing, bank bridging).
Recommendation
Add a worked example in the following format: "Example: $700,000 loan over 12 months at 7.04% p.a.: Interest = $49,280. Plus set-up fee ($5,530 at 0.79%) + valuation ($220) + discharge ($450) = total approx. $55,480. No monthly repayments — all costs settled when your property sells."
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — reduces rate-related drop-off by contextualising cost against bridging loan norms.
A/B Hypothesis
Adding a worked dollar example on the Rates & Fees page will increase the rate of Eligibility and Apply CTAs from this page because users who can self-calculate total cost convert at a higher rate than those left to estimate. Test: Rates & Fees with vs. without worked example. Primary metric: CTA click-through rate from Rates & Fees. Secondary metric: Time on page (engagement with detail).
F-RF-04 No cost framing vs. alternatives ▼
Medium Medium Effort Effort: Medium P02 — Downsizer P04 — Separating
Finding
The page lists costs without framing them against alternatives. For cost-anxious personas (P02 Downsizer, P04 Separating), a cost without context feels large. A bridging loan's total cost vs. "moving twice + storage + short-term rental" or "missing the ideal property and waiting 6 months" reframes the decision.
Recommendation
Add a brief comparison callout: "Compared to renting interim accommodation for 6 months ($X), Bridgit's bridging costs are often competitive — and you only pay at settlement, not month to month."
Impact of Change
Emotional Arc → CTA — increases expert CTA clicks from anxiety-driven personas by reframing cost as certainty.
F-RF-M01 Rates/fees table likely overflows or becomes unreadable at 390px ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium P01 — Upsizer P02 — Downsizer
Finding
HTML contains overflow: hidden declarations (lines 79, 212, 220 of the stylesheet). The rates/fees table contains multiple columns (loan type, interest rate, setup fee, valuation, discharge fee) which cannot fit within a 390px viewport without horizontal scrolling or truncation. P02 (Downsizer) needs to read this table in plain, legible format to make a decision; P01 will check rates quickly on mobile before applying. Both are blocked by an unreadable table.
Recommendation
Implement a responsive table transformation for mobile: convert the multi-column table into stacked card/list rows (label:value pairs per fee item) below 768px. Alternatively, use a horizontally scrollable container with a visible scroll affordance ("← swipe to see more"). Test at 390px to confirm all fee data is accessible.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Trust — increases trust and engagement by making fee table scannable at 390px.
A/B Hypothesis
A mobile-friendly stacked fee layout will increase Rates & Fees page engagement and reduce drop-off before reaching the Apply CTA.
