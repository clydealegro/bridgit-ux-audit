The sections audited here — hero layout, CTA copy, "How it works", "What's next for you?", calculator placement, FAQ accordion, and trust modules — are shared across every page in this template set. Page Context
This is Bridgit's head-and-shoulders keyword page — the highest-traffic, highest-authority page in the SEO content template set, targeting high-volume educational queries such as "what is a bridging loan" and "bridging loan Australia". It is the primary organic SEO entry point for users in the awareness and research phase of the decision funnel. The page sits at /bridgit/what-is-bridging-loan within the Webflow CMS collection (data-wf-collection), indicating it is one of multiple CMS-generated content pages sharing the same underlying template. Additional topic pages in the same collection likely target adjacent queries (e.g., "how does a bridging loan work", "bridging loan costs", etc.). Traffic intent is informational (TOFU), with a minority of commercial-intent visitors. The page contains: educational rich-text content (unique per page), scenario-matching cards, an embedded loan calculator, a "What's next for you?" solution grid, trust signals, and a lead-gen CTA section — the majority of which are template components shared with all other collection pages.
🔄 Funnel Journey Audit
The query this page targets — "what is a bridging loan" — is informational intent, placing it at TOFU (awareness) in the funnel. Important: funnel stage is determined by the page's search query topic, not by the template type. The same H&S template could host MOFU pages ("how does a bridging loan work in practice?") or BOFU pages ("bridging loan rates comparison") — each would require a different journey architecture. For this specific page, the ideal journey is: Land & Orient → Educate → Self-qualify → Guide to next step → Convert.
Stage Status Problem
Land & Orient ❌ Broken "Apply now" CTA immediately signals "we think you're ready to apply" — but the user came to learn what a bridging loan even is. Trust gap from word one.
Educate ✅ Good Rich content exists and answers the question well. This is the page's strongest stage.
Self-qualify ⚠️ Weak 7 scenario cards exist but Hick's Law kills it — no decision help, no "not sure?" exit. Users who don't immediately see themselves in a card bounce.

Convert ❌ Buried Calculator is at 70–80% scroll depth. The one interactive conversion tool is the furthest thing from the user.
Core funnel problem: The page educates well but then drops the user off a cliff. There's no guided handoff from "I now understand bridging loans" to "here's my specific next step."

F-SEO-HS-01 "Apply now" CTA misaligned with informational search intent; campaign UTMs hardcoded into organic page URL ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Low All Personas 🧩 Template scope Stage: Land & Orient Stage: Convert
Finding
The hero CTA is "Apply now" — a bottom-of-funnel action pushed to users who searched "what is a bridging loan", an unambiguously informational (TOFU) query. Users at this stage are building awareness, not ready to apply. An immediate apply CTA creates a trust gap and premature pressure. Additionally, the Apply button URL contains hardcoded campaign UTM parameters: utm_campaign=ScenarioSpotlight&utm_content=March. All organic SEO traffic landing on this page will be incorrectly attributed in analytics to a March email campaign, corrupting channel attribution and making performance measurement unreliable.
Recommendation
Replace the primary hero CTA with a mid-funnel action that matches informational intent: "Check if I qualify" (links to Eligibility) or "See my options" (links to Calculator). Reserve "Apply now" for the conversion section lower on the page. Separately, remove the hardcoded campaign UTM parameters from the Apply CTA href — UTM tracking should be applied dynamically via GTM or at the campaign send level, not baked into a permanent page URL.
Impact of Change
Attribution accuracy + conversion rate — fixes analytics data corruption and reduces early-funnel CTA bounce by matching action to intent. Estimated improvement: 10–20% reduction in hero bounce rate for organic traffic.
A/B Hypothesis
Replacing "Apply now" with "Check if I qualify" as the hero CTA on the H&S SEO page will increase scroll depth and downstream calculator/eligibility engagement because TOFU visitors need to understand their fit before committing to an apply flow. Test: Current "Apply now" hero CTA vs. "Check if I qualify" linking to Eligibility. Primary metric: Eligibility page visit rate from SEO landing. Secondary metric: Session duration, bounce rate.
F-SEO-HS-02 No guided journey for educational visitors — "What's next for you?" presents 7 options creating choice paralysis ▼
High Med. Effort Effort: Medium All Personas 🧩 Template scope Stage: Guide to next step
Finding
After consuming the educational content, visitors have no guided conversion path. The "What's next for you?" section presents 7 equal-weight scenario options (Upsize, Downsize, Renovate, Retire, Separate, Invest, Builder). Hick's Law states that decision time grows logarithmically with the number of choices. For a first-time visitor who just learned what a bridging loan is, 7 undifferentiated options generate cognitive overload — the likely outcome is no selection. There is also no explicit "bridge" between the educational content and the commercial intent section; the transition is abrupt.
Recommendation
Add a brief 2-question scenario selector above "What's next for you?": "Are you buying before selling, or renovating?" and "Which best describes your situation?". Reduce visible options to 3–4 most common scenarios with a "See all" expansion. Alternatively, add a persistent "not sure?" link to the Calculator as a confidence-building fallback.
Impact of Change
Consideration → conversion — reduces drop-off at the scenario selection stage by simplifying the decision tree. Estimated improvement: 15–25% increase in scenario card CTR. 🧩 Template scope: the "What's next for you?" component is shared across all CMS collection pages — one template edit resolves the choice paralysis issue simultaneously for every topic page.
F-SEO-HS-03 H1 "What is Bridging Loan" is grammatically incomplete and inconsistent with meta title phrasing ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low All Personas Stage: Land & Orient
Finding
The page H1 reads "What is Bridging Loan" — missing the article "a" making it grammatically incorrect ("What is a Bridging Loan" is correct English). The meta title uses the same incomplete form. The in-page sub-heading then reads "What Is Bridging Loan and How to Maximise It?" — again missing the article. For a page targeting the exact-match query "what is a bridging loan", the missing article is a semantic mismatch that may reduce relevance scoring for the full query. It also creates a minor but visible quality signal issue for users reading the heading.
Recommendation
Correct all instances: H1 → "What is a Bridging Loan", meta title → "What is a Bridging Loan | Bridgit", sub-heading → "What Is a Bridging Loan and How to Maximise It?". This is a one-line Webflow CMS edit with no design impact.
Impact of Change
SEO relevance + user trust — improves query-to-H1 match for "what is a bridging loan" searches and removes a quality signal issue visible to readers.
F-SEO-HS-04 Loan calculator positioned below 4+ scroll depths — highest-intent conversion tool is buried ▼
Medium Med. Effort Effort: Medium All Personas 🧩 Template scope Stage: Convert
Finding
The embedded loan calculator ("Your bridging point is…") appears below the hero, educational content, scenario-fit section, "How it works" process, "What's next for you?" grid, and "Your bridging experts" trust sections. This positions the calculator at approximately the 70–80% scroll depth point on the page. Surface a mini calculator widget or a prominent "Calculate my bridging loan" CTA within the first 2 scroll depths, ideally directly after the "What is a Bridging Loan?" educational section when user understanding peaks. At minimum, add a sticky "Calculate" button in the page navigation or a floating CTA for users who have scrolled past 50%.
Impact of Change
Consideration → lead gen — surfaces the calculator at the moment of maximum educational engagement, estimated to increase calculator usage by 20–30% from organic SEO traffic. 🧩 Template scope: calculator placement is defined in the template section order — surfacing a calculator widget earlier applies across all CMS collection pages simultaneously.
F-SEO-HS-05 FAQ answers hidden in accordion — visible Q&A content unavailable for Google rich snippet extraction ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low All Personas 🧩 Template scope Stage: Educate
Finding
FAQ content is present in the page but rendered entirely inside collapsed Webflow accordion components (.faq4_accordion). No FAQ question or answer text is visible in the default page state. Google's FAQ rich snippet eligibility requires that "FAQPage" structured data markup be present AND that the Q&A content is accessible to users on the page — not just in hidden markup. Accordion-collapsed FAQs reduce the likelihood of FAQ rich snippets appearing in SERP. This is a missed opportunity on a high-visibility educational keyword page where "People Also Ask" box coverage is critical.
Recommendation
Ensure FAQ structured data (FAQPage JSON-LD schema) is implemented with all questions and answers. Show the first 2–3 FAQ answers in expanded (open) state by default so visible content matches schema content. Alternatively, use a CSS "open" accordion state for the top 3 FAQs and collapse the rest. Verify the structured data passes Google's Rich Results Test.
Impact of Change
SEO organic visibility — enables FAQ rich snippet coverage in SERP, potentially increasing click-through rate by 20–30% for informational queries where PAA (People Also Ask) boxes are present. 🧩 Template scope: the FAQ accordion component is shared across all collection pages — implementing FAQPage schema and default-open state once resolves the rich snippet gap for every topic page in the collection.
F-SEO-HS-06 "From what is, to what's next" heading is ambiguous wordplay that obscures content navigation intent ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Low All Personas 🧩 Template scope Stage: Educate
Finding
The first major section below the hero is headlined "From what is, to what's next" — a wordplay title referencing both the page's educational purpose ("what is") and the commercial next step ("what's next"). While clever, this heading fails as a navigation landmark: it doesn't communicate what the section contains, what the user should do, or how it relates to the page topic. For screen-reader users and keyboard navigators, this heading provides zero orientation value. For SEO, it has no keyword relevance. It creates a cognitive pause before users understand the section purpose.
Recommendation
Replace with a clear, intent-matching heading such as "Understanding Bridging Loans" or "Start Here: What Is a Bridging Loan?" that signals the educational content that follows. The subsection heading "What Is Bridging Loan and How to Maximise It?" is more descriptive and could be promoted to the primary H2 position.
Impact of Change
UX clarity + SEO — improves section scan-ability for all visitors, accessibility for screen reader users, and keyword relevance for the target query cluster. 🧩 Template scope: this section heading is a template-level string — one Webflow edit clears the ambiguous wordplay across every SEO collection page simultaneously.
F-SEO-HS-M01 Hero Apply CTA below fold at 390px — trust badges interrupt visual hierarchy before value proposition is read ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low All Personas 🧩 Template scope Stage: Land & Orient
Finding
On mobile (390px viewport), the hero section layout stacks: headline → trust badges ("Apply Eligibility / Get Approved / Buy First / Sell Freely") → CTA button. The trust badges are process steps, not benefit statements, and occupy significant vertical space between the headline and the CTA. For an educational SEO landing page, where mobile bounce rates are already high, pushing the primary action below the fold reduces the already-low conversion intent for TOFU visitors. The hero headline "Buy your next property on your terms" is benefit-led but the interrupting badge strip weakens the message flow.
Recommendation
On mobile, collapse or reduce the trust badge strip to a single inline line or remove it from the hero entirely on SEO landing pages. Move the primary CTA immediately below the headline. If process badges must be shown, show them below the CTA as supporting information rather than between headline and action.
Impact of Change
Mobile conversion — tightens the headline-to-CTA distance, estimated to improve mobile hero engagement rate by 10–15% for organic SEO visitors. 🧩 Template scope: the mobile hero badge strip layout is a shared template component — reordering it once improves the headline-to-CTA flow across all SEO collection pages.
F-SEO-HS-M02 "What's next for you?" 7-card solution grid creates excessive vertical scroll length on mobile ▼
Medium Med. Effort Effort: Medium All Personas 🧩 Template scope Stage: Guide to next step
Finding
On mobile, the "What's next for you?" solution grid renders all 7 scenario cards (Upsize, Downsize, Renovate, Retire, Separate, Invest, Builder) in a stacked vertical layout, significantly extending page length. Combined with the educational content above and the calculator section below, this creates a very long scroll journey. Mobile users on SEO pages typically have high bounce rates and low patience — an extensive grid section mid-page increases the probability of abandonment before reaching the conversion section.
Recommendation
On mobile, reduce the initial display to 3 cards (the 3 most common scenarios based on analytics: Upsize, Downsize, Renovate) with a "Show all 7 scenarios" expand toggle. Alternatively, use a horizontal scroll carousel for the scenario cards rather than a vertical stack.
Impact of Change
Mobile UX — reduces total page length, improves scroll-to-conversion rate, and keeps the most relevant options visible without overwhelming the layout. 🧩 Template scope: the "What's next for you?" card grid is a shared template component — collapsing it to 3 visible cards on mobile reduces scroll length across every SEO collection page at once.
