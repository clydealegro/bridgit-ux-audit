Solution: Downsize

Personas: P02 — Downsizing Empty Nester 2 desktop findings · 3 mobile findings

F-SOL-ALL-01 Testimonials are generic across all solution pages; not scenario-specific ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
All 7 solution pages include a Trustpilot/testimonial widget section. The same testimonial carousel component appears to be reused across pages without scenario-specific customisation. A user on the Downsize page should see a testimonial from someone who successfully downsized; generic testimonials do not reinforce the persona's decision at the point they need it most.
Recommendation
Source and display at least one scenario-specific testimonial per solution page, placed above the generic carousel. Format: customer name, scenario label (e.g., "Margaret, Downsizer — Brisbane"), 1–2 sentence quote referencing their specific downsizing situation.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases apply rate across all solution pages by replacing generic testimonials with scenario-matched social proof.
F-SOL-ALL-02 No worked examples with real dollar amounts on any solution page ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
No solution page contains a worked scenario with concrete numbers. P02 (Downsizer) cannot mentally model "what would this actually cost me?" without an example. This is one of the most common conversion blockers in high-consideration financial products.
Recommendation
Add a scenario-specific worked example for the Downsize case. Show the bridge amount, interest cost, total cost, and the "no monthly repayments" benefit in plain language suited to P02's level of financial literacy.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — reduces uncertainty-driven abandonment by grounding each solution in a concrete cost or outcome example.
F-SOL-DN-M01 Phone CTA not prominent above fold; blocks P02's primary conversion path ▼
Critical Strategic Effort: Medium P02 — Downsizer
Finding
P02 (62F, retired teacher) converts almost exclusively via phone. Phone number is in the schema but there is no large, tappable "Call us" button in the hero section or sticky header of the Downsize page. For this demographic, the phone CTA must be the most prominent element on mobile — not buried in navigation or visible only after scrolling. Without it above fold, P02's primary conversion path is effectively blocked.
Recommendation
Add a sticky bottom bar or large hero-positioned button: "Call us — 1300 141 161" with tel:1300141161, minimum 48–56px height. Label it "Speak to a specialist" or "Call our team" (plain language, not CTA-jargon). Ensure it remains visible on scroll via position: sticky; bottom: 0 or as a floating action button.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Call — restores P02's primary conversion path (phone) by making tap-to-call prominent above fold.
A/B Hypothesis
A sticky or above-fold phone CTA will significantly increase phone contact rate from P02 on the Downsize page.
F-SOL-DN-M02 Plain-language benefits not visible before first scroll ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Medium P02 — Downsizer
Finding
The hero section is substantial in height; the benefits/features section that speaks to P02's key concerns (simple process, no jargon, clear next steps) is pushed below fold. P02 needs immediate reassurance — a sense of "this is for me" — before they invest time scrolling. Without this, high bounce is likely.
Recommendation
Condense hero on mobile to ~60–70vh. Move a 3–5 bullet "Why Bridgit" callout (plain language: "One phone call to get started", "No monthly repayments", "Move at your own pace") immediately below the hero, above the fold on 390px.
Impact of Change
Emotional Arc → Consideration — reduces P02 bounce by surfacing plain-language benefits before first scroll.
A/B Hypothesis
Surfacing plain-language benefits within the initial viewport will reduce bounce and increase scroll depth for P02 on mobile.
F-SOL-DN-M03 Financial jargon may appear without plain-language tooltip on mobile ▼
Medium Medium Effort Effort: Medium P02 — Downsizer
Finding
Page schema includes "LVR up to 80%" and possibly other finance terms. Tippy.js tooltips are loaded but tooltips activated by hover do not function on mobile touch interfaces. P02 (non-financial background) encountering "LVR", "residual balance", or "settlement period" without an explanation on mobile will either not understand or lose trust.
Recommendation
(1) Replace jargon with plain language wherever possible on the Downsize page. (2) Where technical terms must be used, trigger Tippy tooltips on click/tap events, not hover. (3) Add a plain-language glossary link in the page footer.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Trust — reduces cognitive load and abandonment for P02 by replacing jargon with plain language.
