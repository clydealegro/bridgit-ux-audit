F-SOL-ALL-01 Testimonials are generic across all solution pages; not scenario-specific ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
All 7 solution pages include a Trustpilot/testimonial widget section reused across pages without scenario-specific customisation. A user on the House & Land page should see a testimonial from someone who navigated a build delay or H&L package using Bridgit — generic testimonials don't address P05's specific fears about construction timelines.
Recommendation
Source and display at least one scenario-specific testimonial per solution page. For House & Land, the testimonial should reference a build delay being managed successfully. Format: customer name, scenario label (e.g., "Ryan, H&L Builder — Queensland"), 1–2 sentence quote referencing their build experience.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases apply rate across all solution pages by replacing generic testimonials with scenario-matched social proof.
F-SOL-ALL-02 No worked examples with real dollar amounts on any solution page ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
No solution page contains a worked scenario with concrete numbers. P05 (project engineer) is detail-oriented and needs a cost model before committing. Without a worked H&L example, P05 cannot validate whether Bridgit is financially sensible vs. alternative holding strategies.
Recommendation
For H&L: "Own a $1M home (owe $350K). H&L package = $750K. Bridge $450K for 18 months at 7.04% p.a. = approx. $47,520 interest + fees. Compare to: 18 months rent ($3,000/month) = $54,000 + double-move costs." Add this as a callout card on the page.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — reduces uncertainty-driven abandonment by grounding each solution in a concrete cost or outcome example.
F-SOL-HL-01 No mention of build delays or loan extension flexibility ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium P05 — Builder
Finding
The #1 fear of a House & Land buyer is: "What if my build is delayed?" Construction delays of 3–12 months are common in the current environment. The H&L page does not address this at all — there is no mention of what happens if a build runs late, whether the bridging loan can be extended, or whether Bridgit has experience with delayed H&L settlements. This omission is likely causing significant consideration-stage abandonment.
Recommendation
Add a FAQ or callout: "What if my build is delayed? Our bridging loans offer flexible terms up to 24 months. If your build runs late, we work with you — contact your BDM to discuss."
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases P05 apply rate by explicitly addressing builder delay risk.

F-SOL-HL-M01 Build delay content not visible above fold; P05's primary concern unaddressed ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium P05 — Builder
Finding
P05 (project engineer, on-site phone research) lands on this page specifically to understand how Bridgit handles build delays — their primary risk. FAQ content addressing build delay scenarios ("What happens if my build is delayed?" and "Can I extend the bridge period?") is not confirmed above fold at 390px. F-SOL-HL-M04 Sticky/fixed CSS layout risk on low-connectivity mobile devices ▼
Medium Strategic Effort: High P05 — Builder
Finding
HTML source contains multiple position: fixed and position: sticky declarations (lines 673, 1358–1372) with inline comments referencing fixes for "flash" and "fixed height" rendering issues on the sticky navbar. On mobile with slow connectivity (P05 on a construction site, Slow 4G), these layout behaviours can produce Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) or content momentarily obscuring below the fixed navbar. This creates visual noise that reduces trust for an already time-pressured persona.
Recommendation
(1) Test sticky navbar on 390px with Chrome DevTools throttled to Slow 4G. (2) Verify no content is obscured by the fixed navbar (minimum 60px buffer). (3) Confirm CLS score ≤0.1 for the page. (4) If CLS issues are found, refactor fixed positioning to use IntersectionObserver instead.
Impact of Change
Reliability — reduces layout shift and render issues for P05 in low-connectivity environments.
