Page Audit

Solution: Renovate

Personas: P03 — Equity-Unlock Renovator 3 desktop findings · 3 mobile findings
● 4 High ● 2 Medium
F-SOL-ALL-01 Testimonials are generic across all solution pages; not scenario-specific ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
All 7 solution pages include a Trustpilot/testimonial widget section reused across pages without scenario-specific customisation. A user on the Renovate page should see a testimonial from someone who successfully renovated using Bridgit equity — generic testimonials do not reinforce the persona's decision at the point they need it most.
Recommendation
Source and display at least one scenario-specific testimonial per solution page, placed above the generic carousel. Format: customer name, scenario label (e.g., "Alex, Renovator — Sydney"), 1–2 sentence quote referencing their specific renovation situation.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases apply rate across all solution pages by replacing generic testimonials with scenario-matched social proof.
F-SOL-ALL-02 No worked examples with real dollar amounts on any solution page ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
No solution page contains a worked scenario with concrete numbers. Analytical P03 (Architect/SBO) cannot mentally model "what would this actually cost me?" without an example. This is one of the most common conversion blockers in high-consideration financial products.
Recommendation
For Renovate: "You own a $900K home (owe $400K). You want $150K to renovate. Bridgit can bridge $150K from your equity — here's what it costs." Add this worked example as a callout card on the page.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — reduces uncertainty-driven abandonment by grounding each solution in a concrete cost or outcome example.
F-SOL-REN-01 No explicit comparison to construction loans or refinancing ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium P03 — Renovator
Finding
P03's primary consideration question is "Why use Bridgit over a construction loan, or refinancing my existing mortgage?" The Renovate page doesn't address this. The schema mentions "cosmetic improvements and non-structural renovations" which implies construction loans are out of scope, but no explicit comparison is made. P03 (Architect/SBO) is analytically minded and will compare options before deciding.
Recommendation
Add a comparison section or FAQ: "Why not just refinance? Refinancing can take months and may change your existing rate. Bridgit lets you access equity in as little as 24 hours without touching your current mortgage."
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases P03 apply rate by clarifying how Bridgit funds staged renovations.
A/B Hypothesis
Adding a "Why Bridgit vs. refinancing" comparison section will increase conversion rate on the Renovate page because P03 makes evidence-based decisions and currently has no reason to choose Bridgit over alternatives without this context. Test: With vs. without comparison section. Primary metric: CTA click-through rate on Renovate page. Secondary metric: Time on page.
F-SOL-REN-M01 "Rates & Fees" link not visible above fold; blocks cost-focused P03 ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Low P03 — Renovator
Finding
P03 (architect, SBO) is financially literate and will research costs before applying. A "Rates & Fees" link is not visible in the above-fold section or the hero of the Renovate page at 390px. P03 must either scroll, navigate away, or abandon. Without seeing rates early, P03 cannot complete their due diligence on mobile in a single session.
Recommendation
Add a "View Rates & Fees" text link (with chevron arrow) in the hero section or in a utility strip immediately below the hero. Minimum 44px tap target. Link to /rates-fees. Use a contrasting accent colour to make it scannable without being a primary CTA.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Trust — increases P03 confidence and reduces cost-research bounce by surfacing Rates & Fees link above fold.
A/B Hypothesis
A visible Rates & Fees link above fold will increase engagement from cost-research-driven P03 and reduce navigation abandonment on the Renovate page.
F-SOL-REN-M02 Process steps not visible without scrolling ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Medium P03 — Renovator
Finding
P03 needs to see the loan process clearly — what happens after they apply, how long it takes, when funds are released. The "how it works" or process steps section requires scrolling past the hero on mobile. For a time-conscious business owner who may be doing a 90-second mobile research check, this information is not surfaced quickly enough.
Recommendation
Add a condensed 4-step visual timeline card immediately below the hero at mobile: "1. Apply (5 min) → 2. Property check (24 hr) → 3. Approved → 4. Funds released". Use icons + short labels. Keep to 4 lines max so it is fully visible without scrolling at 390px.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases P03 apply rate by making process timeline visible without scrolling.
A/B Hypothesis
A visible process timeline in the immediate post-hero zone will increase P03 confidence in timeline and reduce application form abandonment on mobile.
F-SOL-REN-M03 No Calculator link in hero or early section ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low P03 — Renovator
Finding
P03 is likely to use the Calculator to model their renovation loan before applying. There is no visible Calculator link in the hero or initial section at 390px. P03 must scroll or navigate away — increasing drop-off risk.
Recommendation
Add a secondary "Use our calculator" text link below the primary Apply button in the hero section (outline style, distinct from primary CTA).
Impact of Change
Consideration → Tool use — increases calculator engagement from cost-conscious P03.
