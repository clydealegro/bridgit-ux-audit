Page Audit

Solution: Separate

Personas: P04 — Separating Homeowner 3 desktop findings · 3 mobile findings
● 4 High ● 2 Medium ● 1 Low
F-SOL-ALL-01 Testimonials are generic across all solution pages; not scenario-specific ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
All 7 solution pages include a Trustpilot/testimonial widget section reused across pages without scenario-specific customisation. A user on the Separate page should see a testimonial from someone who went through separation and used Bridgit to achieve a clean break — generic testimonials do not carry the weight needed for this high-anxiety persona.
Recommendation
Source and display at least one scenario-specific testimonial per solution page. For the Separate page specifically, the testimonial must include a reference to discretion, a clean outcome, and no mention of needing partner co-operation. Format: customer name, scenario label (e.g., "Chris, Separating Homeowner — Melbourne").
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases apply rate across all solution pages by replacing generic testimonials with scenario-matched social proof.
F-SOL-ALL-02 No worked examples with real dollar amounts on any solution page ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
No solution page contains a worked scenario with concrete numbers. P04 (Separating Homeowner) is under financial stress and needs to see a worked example that confirms Bridgit is financially viable for their situation — not theoretical percentages.
Recommendation
Add a worked example for the separation scenario, framed sensitively: "You and your partner jointly own a $900K home with a $300K mortgage. You can access up to $465K in equity through Bridgit to secure your next property before the family home sells." Keep it human and outcome-focused, not transactional.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — reduces uncertainty-driven abandonment by grounding each solution in a concrete cost or outcome example.
F-SOL-SEP-02 No confidentiality or privacy assurance on Separate page ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Low P04 — Separating
Finding
P04 (Separating Homeowner) fears judgment and intrusive questions. The Separate page contains no language explicitly reassuring users that the process is private, judgment-free, and does not require partner involvement upfront. Words like "confidential", "discreet", "no partner details required at this stage" are entirely absent from detectable content.
Recommendation
Add a trust/reassurance statement near the primary CTA: "We handle separation cases with discretion. No details about your partner are required to get started. Our team is here to help — not to ask difficult questions."
Impact of Change
Emotional Arc → CTA — increases P04 expert CTA clicks and reduces abandonment by adding confidentiality assurance.
A/B Hypothesis
Adding confidentiality/discretion language near the CTA on the Separate page will increase "Connect with an expert" clicks because P04's primary hesitation is judgment and loss of privacy, not cost or eligibility. Test: With vs. without confidentiality assurance copy. Primary metric: "Connect with an expert" click-through rate on Separate page. Secondary metric: Bounce rate.
F-SOL-SEP-M01 Apply CTA appears before trust is established in hero ▼
Medium Medium Effort Effort: Low P04 — Separating
Finding
The sticky header displays "Apply Now" immediately alongside the empathetic headline. For P04 (high anxiety, researching privately, emotionally stressed), leading with an Apply CTA before delivering scenario-specific trust content may feel presumptive and increase bounce. The emotional arc should build trust before asking for commitment.
Recommendation
Consider making "Speak to an expert" the primary above-fold sticky CTA for the Separate page specifically, with "Apply" as secondary. Alternatively, delay the Apply CTA to appear after a single scroll (below the empathy section).
Impact of Change
Emotional Arc → Consideration — reduces P04 abandonment by ensuring empathy and trust precede the Apply CTA.
A/B Hypothesis
Testing "Speak to an expert" as the primary sticky CTA vs "Apply Now" on the Separate page will reveal which approach generates higher intent actions from high-anxiety P04.
F-SOL-SEP-M02 No explicit confidentiality signal in above-fold content ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Low P04 — Separating
Finding
P04 is researching in a highly sensitive context. The hero headline is empathetic but includes no explicit confidentiality or privacy signal above fold: no "Private process", "Confidential review", or "Discreet service" micro-copy. Phase 3 finding F-SOL-SEP-02 identified this as a desktop gap — it is equally or more critical on mobile where P04 may be researching from a shared device or public space.
Recommendation
Add a single-line trust signal below or adjacent to the hero headline: "Private & confidential review process" with a lock icon. Maximum 5 words to keep it scannable on mobile.
Impact of Change
Consideration → CTA — increases P04 expert CTA engagement by adding explicit confidentiality signal above fold.
A/B Hypothesis
Adding "Private & confidential review process" to the Separate page hero will increase scroll depth and expert CTA engagement from P04 on mobile.
F-SOL-SEP-M03 Sticky Apply CTA risks banner blindness for P04 ▼
Low Medium Effort Effort: Medium P04 — Separating
Finding
Persistent sticky CTAs on mobile are susceptible to banner blindness, particularly for users in a high-anxiety state (P04) who are reading carefully rather than scanning for actions. The sticky Apply CTA may be mentally filtered out.
Recommendation
A/B test: Sticky header variant A ("Apply Now") vs. variant B ("Speak to an expert" phone link). For the Separate page, the softer expert CTA may convert P04 at a higher rate than the direct Apply button.
Impact of Change
Consideration → CTA — may increase P04 expert CTA clicks by testing softer sticky CTA variant.
A/B Hypothesis
Sticky "Speak to an expert" CTA will generate higher engagement from P04 than sticky "Apply Now" due to lower friction for a high-anxiety separation persona.
