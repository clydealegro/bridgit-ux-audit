F-SOL-ALL-01 Testimonials are generic across all solution pages; not scenario-specific ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
All 7 solution pages include a Trustpilot/testimonial widget section. Based on the HTML structure, the same testimonial carousel component appears to be reused across pages without scenario-specific customisation. A user on the Upsize page should see a testimonial from someone who successfully upsized; generic testimonials do not reinforce the persona's decision at the point they need it most.
Recommendation
Source and display at least one scenario-specific testimonial per solution page, placed above the generic carousel. Format: customer name, scenario label (e.g., "James, Upsizer — Melbourne"), 1–2 sentence quote referencing their specific situation.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases apply rate across all solution pages by replacing generic testimonials with scenario-matched social proof.
A/B Hypothesis
Scenario-specific testimonials on solution pages will increase "Apply now" / "Connect with an expert" click-through rates because visitors self-identify with peers who share their specific situation. Test: Generic carousel vs. scenario-specific testimonial + carousel (per page). Primary metric: Primary CTA click-through rate on solution pages. Secondary metric: Scroll depth past testimonial section.
F-SOL-ALL-02 No worked examples with real dollar amounts on any solution page ▼
High Medium Effort Effort: Medium All Personas
Finding
No solution page contains a worked scenario with concrete numbers. Analytical borrowers (P01 Upsizer, P03 Renovator, P06 Investor) cannot mentally model "what would this actually cost me?" without an example. This is one of the most common conversion blockers in high-consideration financial products — users need to see a real scenario before they commit to applying.
Recommendation
For Upsize: "You own a $1.2M home (owe $500K). You want to buy a $1.75M home. Bridgit can bridge $700K — here's what it costs." Add this worked example as a callout card on the page.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — reduces uncertainty-driven abandonment by grounding each solution in a concrete cost or outcome example.
F-SOL-UP-01 H1 "Move up with Bridgit" is aspirational but not scenario-specific enough ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low P01 — Upsizer
Finding
P01's primary anxiety is "I might miss out on my dream home because I haven't sold yet." The H1 "Move up with Bridgit" doesn't address this fear directly. Compare to a more conversion-specific H1: "Found your dream home? Don't wait for your current one to sell." The current headline is an aspiration statement; the alternative speaks directly to the moment of decision.
Recommendation
A/B test current H1 vs. fear-of-missing-out framing: "Don't let an unsold home stop you from buying the right one." or "Buy your next home before your current one sells."
Impact of Change
Consideration → CTA — increases P01 CTA clicks by quantifying the upgrade benefit in the headline.
F-SOL-UP-02 No bank timeline comparison; 24-hour advantage not dramatised ▼
Medium Medium Effort Effort: Medium P01 — Upsizer
Finding
P01 (Confident Upsizer) has likely considered or been rejected by a bank. Bridgit's 24-hour approval vs. a bank's 2–4 week process is a decisive differentiator. This comparison is not visualised on the Upsize page. A simple side-by-side ("Bridgit: approval in 24 hours | Bank bridging: 2–4 weeks, often unavailable") would directly address P01's bank comparison frame.
Recommendation
Add a comparison table or callout showing Bridgit approval time vs. traditional bank bridging timelines.
Impact of Change
Consideration → Apply — increases P01 apply rate by addressing simultaneous mortgage concern.
F-SOL-UP-M01 Hero content extends beyond initial mobile viewport ▼
High Quick Win Effort: Low P01 — Upsizer
Finding
Screenshot shows the hero section extends beyond the initial 390px viewport. The primary CTA may not be fully visible without scrolling. For P01 (mobile-primary, high anxiety about missing out on a property), the CTA must be reachable immediately without scroll to capture high-intent visits.
Recommendation
Reduce hero section vertical height on mobile: set max-height: 90vh on .section_hero-main or reduce padding in the hero container to ensure headline + subhead + CTA are all visible at 390px without scrolling.
Impact of Change
CTA conversion — increases P01 apply clicks by surfacing headline and CTA within initial mobile viewport.
A/B Hypothesis
Reducing hero height to fit CTA above fold on mobile will increase immediate apply click-through for P01.
F-SOL-UP-M02 No "Speak to Expert" or phone CTA visible above fold ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Medium P01 — Upsizer
Finding
While tel: links are present in the page, no prominent "Speak to a specialist" or tap-to-call button is visible in the above-fold hero. P01 may prefer a quick call to confirm speed and process before committing to an application; the absence of a softer CTA option above fold means those users must scroll or apply cold.
Recommendation
Add a secondary CTA in the hero or sticky header: "Call us — 1300 141 161" with tel: href and a 44px minimum tap target. Alternatively, include "Speak to a specialist" as a text link below the primary Apply button.
Impact of Change
Consideration → CTA — provides softer entry point for P01 considering a call before committing to apply.
A/B Hypothesis
Offering a visible phone CTA alongside Apply in the above-fold zone will capture hesitant P01 users and increase overall hero-to-contact conversion.
F-SOL-UP-M03 Primary CTA copy is generic ("Apply Now") ▼
Medium Quick Win Effort: Low P01 — Upsizer
Finding
The primary CTA button reads "Apply Now" — identical to every other solution page. P01 is on a scenario-specific Upsize page under emotional pressure. Scenario-specific CTA copy ("Apply to upsize" or "Start my upsize application") increases confidence that the form is relevant to her situation. Cross-reference: F-NAV-01 (Phase 2).
Recommendation
Change the primary CTA on the Upsize page from "Apply Now" to "Apply to upsize" or "Start upsizing".
Impact of Change
CTA conversion — increases P01 click-through by making CTA copy specific to the upsize scenario.
A/B Hypothesis
"Apply to upsize" will increase CTA click-through rate vs generic "Apply Now" on the Upsize page, particularly for first-visit mobile users.
