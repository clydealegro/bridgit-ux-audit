P01
The Confident Upsizer
38F, senior marketing manager. Wants to buy a larger home before selling current property to avoid losing a property to a competing buyer. Mobile-primary researcher, high urgency.
● Conversion Path: Partial
Primary Pages
Homepage → Calculator → Eligibility → Apply (Journey J1)
Conversion Goal
Secure financing to buy before selling current home; avoid losing property to a competing buyer

Persona Profile

"We found our dream home — we can't risk losing it"
Claire Montgomery  ·  Senior Marketing Manager  ·  38  ·  Inner West, Sydney  ·  📍 Found a larger family home but hasn't listed current property yet. Worried about missing out if they sell first.
Background & Context
  • Married with 2 school-age children, household income ~$240K
  • Owns a 3-bed terrace worth ~$1.4M, mortgage ~$600K outstanding
  • Wants to upsize to a 4-bed home with backyard in the same suburb (~$1.9M)
  • Strong equity but limited savings buffer after recent renovations
  • Has spoken to their bank but found the process slow and unsatisfying
Goals & Motivations
  • Secure the new home before losing it to another buyer
  • Avoid double-moving and the disruption of temporary rentals
  • Sell current home at optimal price by not rushing the sale
  • Minimise financial stress on the family during the transition
Pain Points
  • Bank took 3+ weeks to respond — property was almost gone
  • Confused about how two mortgages work simultaneously
  • Unsure what they can actually borrow in peak market conditions
  • Doesn't want to make an offer and look foolish if finance falls through
Fears & Objections
  • What if the new property doesn't value up?
  • What if our current home sells for less than expected?
  • Hidden fees or terms that change the deal after signing
  • Being "trapped" in a loan they can't exit easily
Digital Behaviour
  • Heavy smartphone user — researches on mobile during commute
  • Trusts Google reviews, Trustpilot and Reddit over brand claims
  • Watches comparison content on YouTube to understand finance products
  • Would share their research with their partner before acting
Decision Triggers
  • Seeing a clear rate upfront without needing to call
  • A calculator that models the buy-now-sell-later scenario specifically
  • Fast approval timeline stated clearly ("answer within 24 hours")
  • Testimonial from someone in a similar upsizing situation
UX Expectations & CRO Considerations
  • Hero section must immediately address "I want to buy before I sell" — that's their exact framing
  • The eligibility checker should require minimal inputs and give a directional answer fast
  • Show a worked example: "You own $1.4M home, want to buy $1.9M — here's how it works"
  • A progress indicator on the application would reduce abandonment anxiety
  • Side-by-side comparison showing Bridgit vs bank timeline would win this persona decisively
"I don't want to lose this house because my bank moves too slowly. I just need someone to tell me if we can make the numbers work."

Critical Gaps

🚧
Journey fractured at Calculator stage No direct path from calculator result to eligibility checker. "Get a quote" CTA bypasses the self-qualification step, creating abandonment for uncertain users and breaking the documented J1 journey. Finding: F-NAV-05/10
📱
Mobile hero blocks immediate CTA At 390px, the Upsize hero extends beyond the viewport, requiring scroll to reach the primary Apply button. P01 is high-urgency and mobile-primary — this friction creates drop-off when seconds matter. Finding: F-SOL-UP-M01
💰
Cost research friction No embedded calculator on homepage and no quick-access eligibility pre-screener mean P01 must navigate through multiple pages to model their scenario. Lack of worked cost examples on Rates & Fees forces manual calculation. Findings: F-RF-02 F-RF-03
🎯
Weak scenario identity Generic "Apply now" across all pages does not confirm P01 is in the right place. Scenario-specific CTA copy reduces hesitation by an estimated 5–15%. Finding: F-NAV-01 F-SOL-UP-M03

What the Site Does Well

  • Mobile-accessible phone number and tap-to-call links are consistent and prominent in the header.
  • Homepage rotating H1 headlines include "Move up with Bridgit," explicitly addressing the upsizing scenario.
  • Two-CTA hero structure (Apply + Connect with Expert) acknowledges both high-intent and consideration-stage visitors.

F-ELG-01 (upstream)

P01 lands on the homepage anxious about missing out on the property. The rotating headline "Move up with Bridgit" reassures, but testimonials don't mention upsizers and there's no quick way to test eligibility. She finds the calculator and feels empowered, but after calculating, "Get a quote" breaks her intended eligibility-check flow. At Eligibility, dense criteria with jargon and no quick summary increases decision friction. She eventually reaches Apply, but accumulated micro-frictions have reduced her confidence from "I'm ready" to "I hope this is right." The journey takes 8–12 minutes instead of the 3–5 P01 intends to invest.

Overall arc: Starts anxious (2/5) due to fear of missing out on the property, builds to cautious optimism (3/5) after seeing clear messaging, drops sharply to frustration (1/5) at the Calculator → Eligibility gap, recovers to cautious confidence (3/5) at Apply step. The J1 journey is technically possible but broken at the calculator-to-eligibility inflection point. Mobile hero friction and generic CTA copy reduce conversion velocity by an estimated 20–30%.

Highest-ROI Quick Win

⚡
Add "Check my eligibility" secondary CTA to calculator result F-NAV-05 — Expected impact: +15–20% completion rate from calculator users; restores J1 journey flow by bridging the fractured Calculator → Eligibility step that currently causes P01 to abandon or loop back to the start.
