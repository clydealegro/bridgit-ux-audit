P03
The Equity-Unlock Renovator
44M, architect and small business owner. Wants to access equity without breaking a fixed-rate mortgage to fund a renovation. Research-driven, spreadsheet-oriented, cost-comparison focused.
● Conversion Path: Partial
Primary Pages
Bridging Loan → Rates & Fees → Eligibility → Apply (Journey J3)
Conversion Goal
Access equity without breaking fixed-rate mortgage to fund renovation

Critical Gaps

🔗
Due diligence path is friction-heavy Repeated nav dropdown interactions slow research velocity. The in-body link from Bridging Loan → Rates & Fees is missing, forcing a nav dropdown re-visit to continue the natural research sequence. Finding: F-NAV-03
🧮
Lack of renovation-specific cost clarity P03 rebuilds spreadsheets to compare Bridgit's total cost vs. construction loans vs. refinancing. Without a worked example, he must estimate — creating high decision friction at the most critical consideration stage. Finding: F-RF-03
🏗️
Renovation process unclear Does Bridgit fund staged renovations (progress payments) or lump-sum? Does the loan term flex for construction delays? The Renovate page lacks these specifics, creating self-exclusion risk for renovation scenarios. Finding: F-SOL-REN-01
📝
Self-employed eligibility jargon ABN, YTD income, and BAS appear without definitions. While P03 knows these terms, the jargon signals "default product, not tailored for SBO." Finding: F-ELG-04

What the Site Does Well

  • Bridging Loan page has a strong "How it works" section with outcome-focused steps.
  • In-body links to Rates & Fees confirmed on the Renovate page.
  • Scenario-specific Renovate page exists and acknowledges the equity-unlock use case.
  • All pages reachable within 2 clicks via 3-tier dropdown.

P03 arrives at the Bridging Loan page wanting to model if bridging is better than construction loans. He immediately wants rates but must use the nav dropdown (in-body link missing). On Rates & Fees, he sees percentages but no worked renovation example and spends 10–15 minutes in his own spreadsheet — work that a single example would eliminate. He persists because he's analytical, but some P03 visitors abandon mid-due-diligence.

Overall arc: Starts curious (3/5), builds to research confidence (4/5), drops to analytical concern (2/5) when cost details are incomplete, recovers to cautious commitment (3/5–4/5) if he can model his scenario. The J3 journey path exists and is navigable, but micro-frictions stack to slow research velocity. P03 completed applications are estimated 15–25% lower than they could be.

⚡
Add worked cost example to Rates & Fees page F-RF-03 — Expected impact: +10–15% apply rate from cost-research users; eliminates manual spreadsheet work. Example: "$220K renovation bridge — 7.5% p.a., total cost ~$17,500 for 12 months. Paid from sale proceeds." Saves 10–15 minutes per P03 visitor.
