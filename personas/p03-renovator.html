🔨 P03 — The Equity-Unlock Renovator

44M, architect / small business owner, research-driven, cost-focused 10 findings · Conversion path: Partial
🔨
P03
The Equity-Unlock Renovator
44M, architect and small business owner. Wants to access equity without breaking a fixed-rate mortgage to fund a renovation. Research-driven, spreadsheet-oriented, cost-comparison focused.
● Conversion Path: Partial
Primary Pages
Bridging Loan → Rates & Fees → Eligibility → Apply (Journey J3)
Conversion Goal
Access equity without breaking fixed-rate mortgage to fund renovation

Persona Profile

"The bones are great — I just want to transform it without moving"
Leo Bennet  ·  Architect / Small Business Owner  ·  44  ·  Fitzroy, Melbourne  ·  📍 Loves their home and neighbourhood. Wants to do a major extension/renovation but won't refinance their existing low fixed-rate mortgage.
Background & Context
  • Single, owns a 2-bed terrace worth ~$1.1M with ~$250K mortgage at a low fixed rate
  • Wants to fund a $220K renovation (rear extension + kitchen) without breaking the fixed loan
  • Earns ~$130K — creditworthy but wants to preserve cash flow
  • Researched home equity loans and construction loans — found them inflexible
  • Came across bridging finance through a trade magazine or architect peer
Goals & Motivations
  • Access equity without touching the existing mortgage or paying break fees
  • Fund the renovation and retain the home without renting elsewhere
  • A loan that aligns with the construction timeline, not arbitrary terms
  • Understand the financial model clearly before engaging a builder
Pain Points
  • Banks want to refinance the whole loan — they'll lose their great rate
  • Construction loan approval processes are long and builder-dependent
  • No obvious product on the market seems designed for "renovate and stay"
  • The Renovate use case on most lender sites is vague
Fears & Objections
  • Renovation runs over time or budget — does the loan term flex?
  • What happens if valuation comes in lower than expected?
  • Being locked into a product that doesn't fit their actual use case
  • Interest costs making the renovation financially unviable
Digital Behaviour
  • Highly analytical — will build spreadsheets to model scenarios
  • Reads comparison sites, Reddit (/r/AusFinance), and deep-dives product PDFs
  • Will complete the full research journey over 1–2 weeks before converting
  • Active on Instagram but for lifestyle, not finance decisions
Decision Triggers
  • A calculator that models equity access specifically for renovation scenarios
  • A "Renovate" dedicated page with a clear step-by-step process
  • Transparent comparison of costs vs refinancing vs construction loans
  • No-obligation quote that gives ballpark figures without a hard credit check
UX Expectations & CRO Considerations
  • The Renovate page must clearly distinguish Bridgit from a construction loan or refinance product
  • Show the use case clearly: "Stay in your home while renovating — access equity without touching your mortgage"
  • This persona will scrutinise the Rates page — fees and comparison rates must be very clear
  • A downloadable PDF guide or detailed FAQ would match their research style
  • The calculator is make-or-break for this persona — it must handle a "renovate in place" scenario
"I'm not moving. I'm not refinancing my cheap rate. I just want to unlock what's already mine and do this renovation properly."

Critical Gaps

🔗
Due diligence path is friction-heavy Repeated nav dropdown interactions slow research velocity. The in-body link from Bridging Loan → Rates & Fees is missing, forcing a nav dropdown re-visit to continue the natural research sequence. Finding: F-NAV-03
🧮
Lack of renovation-specific cost clarity P03 rebuilds spreadsheets to compare Bridgit's total cost vs. construction loans vs. refinancing. Without a worked example, he must estimate — creating high decision friction at the most critical consideration stage. Finding: F-RF-03
🏗️
Renovation process unclear Does Bridgit fund staged renovations (progress payments) or lump-sum? Does the loan term flex for construction delays? The Renovate page lacks these specifics, creating self-exclusion risk for renovation scenarios. Finding: F-SOL-REN-01
📝
Self-employed eligibility jargon ABN, YTD income, and BAS appear without definitions. While P03 knows these terms, the jargon signals "default product, not tailored for SBO." Finding: F-ELG-04

What the Site Does Well

  • Bridging Loan page has a strong "How it works" section with outcome-focused steps.
  • In-body links to Rates & Fees confirmed on the Renovate page.
  • Scenario-specific Renovate page exists and acknowledges the equity-unlock use case.
  • All pages reachable within 2 clicks via 3-tier dropdown.

P03 arrives at the Bridging Loan page wanting to model if bridging is better than construction loans. He immediately wants rates but must use the nav dropdown (in-body link missing). On Rates & Fees, he sees percentages but no worked renovation example and spends 10–15 minutes in his own spreadsheet — work that a single example would eliminate. He persists because he's analytical, but some P03 visitors abandon mid-due-diligence.

Overall arc: Starts curious (3/5), builds to research confidence (4/5), drops to analytical concern (2/5) when cost details are incomplete, recovers to cautious commitment (3/5–4/5) if he can model his scenario. The J3 journey path exists and is navigable, but micro-frictions stack to slow research velocity. P03 completed applications are estimated 15–25% lower than they could be.

Highest-ROI Quick Win

⚡
Add worked cost example to Rates & Fees page F-RF-03 — Expected impact: +10–15% apply rate from cost-research users; eliminates manual spreadsheet work. Example: "$220K renovation bridge — 7.5% p.a., total cost ~$17,500 for 12 months. Paid from sale proceeds." Saves 10–15 minutes per P03 visitor.
