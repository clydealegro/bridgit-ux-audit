💙
P04
The Separating Homeowner
41F, operations manager. Going through a separation, wants to buy out former partner's share, stay in the family home, and resolve financial entanglement quickly while managing significant emotional stress.
● Conversion Path: Partial
Primary Pages
Separate → Eligibility → Apply or Speak to Expert (Journey J4)
Conversion Goal
Buy out former partner's share; stay in family home; resolve financial entanglement quickly

Persona Profile

"We need to untangle our finances without losing everything"
Natallie Chen  ·  Operations Manager  ·  41  ·  Parramatta, Sydney  ·  📍 Going through separation. Joint property needs to be resolved — wants to stay and buy out the other partner without a forced sale and rebuy.
Background & Context
  • Jointly owns a $1.2M home with ~$400K mortgage — wants to remain in the home
  • Needs to pay out an estimated $300–350K share to former partner
  • Income is ~$105K — stable employment but sole income after separation
  • Emotionally stressed, time-pressured, and doesn't want extra complexity
  • Has been referred by a family lawyer or financial adviser
Goals & Motivations
  • Stay in the family home with the children, avoiding another major upheaval
  • Buy out former partner's share fairly and quickly
  • Get clarity on what they can afford before making legal commitments
  • A process that's discreet, compassionate, and efficient
Pain Points
  • Banks are slow and may decline based on single income assessment
  • Doesn't fully understand how buy-out finance works in a separation context
  • Time-pressure from legal settlement deadlines — can't wait 4–6 weeks
  • Emotionally stretched — doesn't want to feel judged or explain the situation repeatedly
Fears & Objections
  • Being declined because income alone isn't enough
  • Loan terms being misaligned with the settlement timeline
  • Locking into something that prevents moving forward if plans change
  • Having to share private personal/legal details unnecessarily
Digital Behaviour
  • Searches late at night when kids are in bed — emotionally driven sessions
  • Skimming-first reader — bounces if the page doesn't quickly address their situation
  • Highly influenced by whether the brand "feels" trustworthy and human
  • Will not convert on first visit — likely returns 2–3 times before acting
Decision Triggers
  • A "Separate" use case page that acknowledges the emotional context
  • Clear assurance that single or changed income scenarios are handled
  • Confidential, non-judgmental language throughout
  • Quick turnaround language ("decision within 24 hours") to match legal deadlines
UX Expectations & CRO Considerations
  • The Separate page needs empathetic, human-first language — not just product features
  • Avoid terms like "residual balance" and "LVR" — use plain language throughout
  • "Connect with an expert" CTA should signal privacy and care, not just speed
  • Do not ask for partner details upfront — this can feel intrusive and cause drop-off
  • Add a "What happens if my income has changed?" FAQ entry — critical for this segment
"I just need this sorted as quickly and quietly as possible. My solicitor says I need bridging finance — I don't need more stress, I need someone who can just make this work."

Critical Gaps

💬
Headline misses emotional reality The Separate page H1 "Move on without finance holding you back" is transactional. P04 needs the brand to acknowledge the life event before pivoting to product. This framing mismatch causes immediate bounce from the highest-anxiety persona on the site. Finding: F-NAV-06
🔍
Separation scenario invisible in Eligibility The Eligibility page does not mention separation or divorce. P04 asks herself "Is my situation even covered?" and self-excludes before ever engaging with a CTA. Finding: F-ELG-02
🔒
No privacy or confidentiality signal No language like "We handle this sensitively" or "Your details are confidential" appears near the CTAs or on the Separate page. Confidentiality is the single most important trust signal for P04. Findings: F-SOL-SEP-02 F-SOL-SEP-M02
⏰
Jargon + time pressure = drop-off P04 researches late at night under emotional stress. Jargon without plain-language equivalents combined with decision urgency creates high abandonment. Tone and clarity are more important for P04 than any other persona. Findings: F-RF-02 F-RF-04

What the Site Does Well

  • A dedicated Separate solution page exists, signalling the separation scenario is understood by Bridgit.
  • "Connect with an expert" CTA provides a softer entry point for P04 who may not be ready to apply.
  • In-body link from Separate page to Eligibility confirmed, supporting the J4 journey path.

P04 lands on the Separate page late at night after the kids are in bed, emotionally exhausted. She reads "Move on without finance holding you back" and feels instantly alienated — the page talks about finance, not her emotional reality. She searches Eligibility for "separation" and finds nothing. She asks herself "Is my situation even covered?" and self-excludes. If she persists and clicks "Connect with an expert," she hesitates because the previous page was clinical.

Overall arc: Starts anxious and guarded (1/5), drops to despair (1/5) if page tone is clinical, recovers to cautious trust (2/5) if empathy is clear, climbs to hope (3/5) if confidentiality is assured and expert contact is easy. P04 is estimated to have a 30–40% drop-off rate between landing on the Separate page and clicking any CTA. Tone and confidentiality signals are more important for P04 than any other persona on the site.
