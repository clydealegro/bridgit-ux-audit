Critical Gaps

Headline misses emotional reality The Separate page H1 "Move on without finance holding you back" is transactional. P04 needs the brand to acknowledge the life event before pivoting to product. This framing mismatch causes immediate bounce from the highest-anxiety persona on the site. Finding: F-NAV-06

⏰
Jargon + time pressure = drop-off P04 researches late at night under emotional stress. Jargon without plain-language equivalents combined with decision urgency creates high abandonment. Tone and clarity are more important for P04 than any other persona. Findings: F-RF-02 F-RF-04

  • A dedicated Separate solution page exists, signalling the separation scenario is understood by Bridgit.
  • "Connect with an expert" CTA provides a softer entry point for P04 who may not be ready to apply.
  • In-body link from Separate page to Eligibility confirmed, supporting the J4 journey path.

P04 lands on the Separate page late at night after the kids are in bed, emotionally exhausted. She reads "Move on without finance holding you back" and feels instantly alienated — the page talks about finance, not her emotional reality. She searches Eligibility for "separation" and finds nothing. She asks herself "Is my situation even covered?" and self-excludes. If she persists and clicks "Connect with an expert," she hesitates because the previous page was clinical.

Overall arc: Starts anxious and guarded (1/5), drops to despair (1/5) if page tone is clinical, recovers to cautious trust (2/5) if empathy is clear, climbs to hope (3/5) if confidentiality is assured and expert contact is easy. P04 is estimated to have a 30–40% drop-off rate between landing on the Separate page and clicking any CTA. Tone and confidentiality signals are more important for P04 than any other persona on the site.
