📈 P06 — The Property Investor

47M, financial services, time-poor, efficiency-focused 13 findings · Conversion path: Partial
📈
P06
The Property Investor
47M, financial services professional. Wants to move fast on a time-sensitive acquisition, bridging the gap between buying a new investment property and selling an existing one. Time-poor, efficiency-focused, portfolio-aware.
● Conversion Path: Partial
Primary Pages
Invest page → Rates & Fees → Eligibility → Apply
Conversion Goal
Move fast on time-sensitive acquisition; bridge gap between buying new and selling existing investment property

Persona Profile

"Time in the market beats timing the market — I need to move fast"
Financial Services Professional / Private Investor  ·  47  ·  South Yarra, Melbourne  ·  📍 Identifies a time-sensitive investment property opportunity but has equity tied up in existing IPs. Needs to move before sale proceeds arrive.
Background & Context
  • Owns 3 investment properties in addition to PPOR — sophisticated borrower
  • Total portfolio value ~$3.8M, combined debt ~$1.6M
  • Has identified a distressed sale property they want to acquire quickly
  • Needs to bridge the gap between buying new and selling one existing IP
  • Has used bridging finance before through a major bank — found it bureaucratic
Goals & Motivations
  • Move fast on a time-sensitive acquisition without losing the opportunity
  • Avoid liquidating a performing asset at a suboptimal time
  • A lender who understands complex portfolio ownership and investment intent
  • Minimal friction and paperwork — respects their time
Pain Points
  • Most bridging lenders treat investors with the same process as first-timers
  • Complex ownership structures (trusts, SMSF-adjacent) create delays
  • Rates and fees are not always clear for investors vs owner-occupiers
  • Having to educate lenders on their own portfolio scenario is exhausting
Fears & Objections
  • Missing the acquisition window because approval takes too long
  • Rates that aren't competitive for a sophisticated, low-risk borrower
  • A lender who doesn't understand investment property dynamics
  • Exit strategy being misunderstood — they have multiple options, not just one sale
Digital Behaviour
  • Time-poor — wants answers fast, low tolerance for friction
  • Will judge credibility by the quality of the Rates and Eligibility pages
  • May arrive via broker, LinkedIn, or professional referral network
  • Reviews the fine print — comparison rates, fees schedule, early repayment terms
Decision Triggers
  • Clear investor eligibility criteria without having to call to find out
  • A dedicated "Invest" page that speaks to portfolio strategy, not just simple bridge logic
  • Rates page that distinguishes investor vs owner-occupier pricing
  • Fast track or priority review option for straightforward applications
UX Expectations & CRO Considerations
  • The Invest page must speak to portfolio logic, not just the simple "buy before you sell" narrative
  • Add a "suitable for investors?" call-out near the eligibility section
  • Speed and low-friction CTA — this persona is most likely to convert quickly if the product is right
  • Trust signals should include ASIC licence number, volume data, and any industry recognition
  • Don't make this persona scroll through retirement/downsize messaging before reaching relevant content
"I've done this before. I don't need hand-holding — I need a lender who can move as fast as the market does and get out of my way once I've qualified."

Critical Gaps

📋
Portfolio complexity not addressed The Eligibility page does not clarify how complex portfolios (trusts, multiple properties, self-managed super funds) are assessed. P06 self-qualifies quickly and self-excludes if he cannot confirm eligibility without a call. Finding: F-ELG-04
💹
Investor-specific details buried The Invest page should state investor LVR limits, rental income treatment, and rate differences upfront. Without this, P06 must call — friction for a time-poor persona who expects key numbers above the fold. Findings: F-SOL-INV-01 F-SOL-INV-M01 F-SOL-INV-M02
✉️
No async contact option P06 may want to send a quick pre-qualification inquiry without committing to a scheduled call. The complete absence of email contact causes abandonment for a persona who operates on their own schedule. Finding: F-NAV-04
🏆
No volume or credibility stats P06 judges lender credibility by scale: funded volume, approval times, number of investor deals completed, investor testimonials. The homepage and Invest page lack these signals. Finding: F-HP-03

What the Site Does Well

  • A dedicated Invest solution page exists within 2 clicks, signalling investor scenarios are understood.
  • Rates & Fees page distinguishes owner-occupier vs. investor pricing — a key differentiator.
  • Two-CTA hero structure (Apply + Connect) supports both high-intent and consideration-stage paths.

P06 lands on the Invest page expecting to find investor LVR limits, rental income treatment, and rate differentials above the fold. Instead, he finds a generic page with the same Apply CTA as other pages. He navigates to Rates & Fees and sees pricing but no worked investor example. At Eligibility, complex portfolio questions are not addressed. He decides to make contact — and finds only a phone number. As a time-poor professional, he cannot commit to a call during business hours and closes the browser.

P06 has a 25–35% higher drop-off rate on mobile than desktop. The primary cause is the combination of missing investor-specific information above the fold and the complete absence of an async contact option. Invest page is discoverable and pricing distinction is visible, but complex portfolio scenarios are unaddressed.
