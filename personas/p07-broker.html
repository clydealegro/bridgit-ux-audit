🤝 P07 — The Mortgage Broker

39M, accredited broker, needs BDM contact and fast accreditation 6 findings · Conversion path: Partial
🤝
P07
The Mortgage Broker
39M, accredited mortgage broker. Evaluating Bridgit as a new panel lender. Needs to find a state-based BDM, confirm the value proposition, and accredit quickly. Efficiency is paramount — any extra navigation step may prompt a competitor comparison.
● Conversion Path: Partial
Primary Pages
Partners → Find your BDM → Accreditation (Journey J5)
Conversion Goal
Accredit with Bridgit; establish BDM relationship; position Bridgit in client toolkit for bridging scenarios

Persona Profile

"I need a bridging solution I can actually recommend with confidence"
Accredited Mortgage Broker  ·  39  ·  Neutral Bay, Sydney  ·  📍 Manages a 200+ client book with multiple bridging candidates per year. Looking for a reliable, fast, broker-friendly specialist lender to add to their panel.
Background & Context
  • Independent broker writing ~$30M/year in residential loans
  • Has referred to major banks for bridging but is frustrated by slow turnarounds
  • Building a referral network and wants a specialist lender in the panel
  • Has 3–5 active clients right now who might be Bridgit candidates
  • Values their own professional reputation — only recommends products they trust
Goals & Motivations
  • Find a bridging lender they can confidently accredit with and refer clients to
  • Get quick answers on client eligibility without lodging full applications
  • A BDM relationship that actually responds and adds value
  • Streamlined broker portal that doesn't slow down the deal
Pain Points
  • Most bridging lenders have slow credit teams and inconsistent broker comms
  • Hard to explain complex product to clients without clear broker support materials
  • Accreditation processes can be lengthy and their time is valuable
  • Not always confident in how to position bridging vs other solutions
Fears & Objections
  • Recommending a product that lets their client down at settlement
  • Slow approvals creating fallout on time-critical deals
  • Low trail commission or unpredictable fee structures
  • Accrediting with a lender who changes their policy or exits the market
Digital Behaviour
  • Works in a broker aggregator platform (Connective, PLAN, AFG)
  • Researches lenders via aggregator panels, LinkedIn, and broker forums
  • Visits the Partners section specifically — main homepage is secondary
  • Will evaluate the quality of broker support materials before accrediting
Decision Triggers
  • Fast BDM contact response (named BDM with direct number)
  • Clear broker accreditation process with a timeline stated upfront
  • Dedicated broker portal and client scenario calculator
  • CPD-accredited training or "how to position bridging" resources
UX Expectations & CRO Considerations
  • The Partners section is this persona's primary destination — must be as polished as the consumer section
  • "Find Your BDM" tool must be functional, current, and show a human face and contact number
  • A broker calculator or "scenario tool" that helps pre-qualify clients would be a major differentiator
  • Broker-specific testimonials or case studies on the Partners page would build confidence
  • The accreditation sign-up flow should feel fast and professional — not bureaucratic
"If I'm going to put my name to a referral, I need to know the lender is going to deliver. My reputation is on the line every time I make an introduction."

Critical Gaps

👤
BDM discovery is fragmented The Find your BDM tool is a separate page. High-converting broker-facing sites embed a state selector on the Partners page itself, surfacing the BDM name, photo, phone, and email immediately. One extra navigation step signals the company is not broker-first. Finding: F-PTR-02
💬
Broker-specific social proof absent The Partners page shows customer testimonials. P07 needs broker peer validation: "As a broker, I refer to Bridgit because..." Customer testimonials carry no weight in the broker evaluation context. Finding: F-PTR-01
✉️
BDM contact is phone-only No email option means P07, who may be in client meetings, cannot send a quick async inquiry to a BDM. The absence of email or a contact form on the Partners page creates unnecessary friction. Finding: F-PTR-M01
📢
H1 misses the broker value proposition "Partner with Bridgit" is transactional. A benefit-led H1 ("Earn better commissions" or "Fast client approvals — no clawbacks") would immediately communicate the broker-specific value proposition that differentiates Bridgit on the partner panel. Finding: F-PTR-03

What the Site Does Well

  • A dedicated Partners section exists in the main nav, signalling broker engagement is a priority.
  • A separate Find your BDM page exists with state-based filtering.
  • Accreditation flow is documented in the Partners dropdown.
  • Schema data confirms broker benefits: 24-hour approval, no clawbacks, upfront broker fees, trail available.

If he cannot find a BDM contact card on the Partners page and must navigate separately, he feels friction — one extra step suggests the company is not broker-focused.

Overall arc: Starts curious (3/5), drops to frustration (2/5) if BDM contact requires a separate page, recovers to efficiency mindset (3/5–4/5) once BDM is contacted, climbs to confidence (4/5) if BDM is responsive. The entire journey should take 2–3 minutes; any longer and P07 may move on to a competing lender. Estimated impact: 20–30% increase in broker accreditations and deal submissions if BDM discovery is embedded and email contact is added.
