🌏 P08 — The Regional Relocator

36M, remote developer, async-preferring, email over phone 12 findings · Conversion path: Blocked
🌏
P08
The Regional Relocator
36M, remote software developer. Wants to secure a regional property before listing his Sydney home, avoiding double ownership or forced sale pressure. Researches async, prefers email over phone, may have limited mobile connectivity.
● Conversion Path: Blocked
Primary Pages
Homepage → Eligibility → Calculator → Email/async contact (Journey J6)
Conversion Goal
Secure regional property before listing Sydney home; avoid double ownership or forced sale pressure

Persona Profile

"We're leaving the city — but we can't time the market perfectly"
Remote Software Developer  ·  36  ·  Moving from Sydney to Orange, NSW  ·  📍 Post-COVID remote work has enabled a tree-change. Has found the right regional property and wants to buy before listing the Sydney home, which may take longer to sell.
Background & Context
  • Fully remote worker — no geographic constraint to employment
  • Owns a Sydney apartment worth ~$850K with $380K mortgage remaining
  • Wants to buy a regional property for ~$650K outright or with minimal mortgage
  • Concerned Sydney property may sit on market for longer in current conditions
  • First-time bridging finance user, very research-oriented
Goals & Motivations
  • Secure the regional property before the vendor accepts another offer
  • Not be forced to rent in the region while waiting for Sydney to sell
  • Avoid the scenario of owning two full mortgages if Sydney sits unsold
  • Move the family once, cleanly, without the chaos of a staged transition
Pain Points
  • Sydney apartment market is slower — realistic sale timeline is unknown
  • Uncertainty about whether a regional property qualifies under lender policies
  • Small-town properties may have different valuation considerations
  • Feels like all the lenders only think about capital city upsizers
Fears & Objections
  • Sydney property not selling within the bridge loan term
  • Regional property being ineligible or valued poorly by the lender
  • Getting stuck in financial limbo with two properties and no income change
  • Lender not understanding the "tree-change" scenario — feeling like an edge case
Digital Behaviour
  • Very high digital literacy — will audit the lender thoroughly online
  • Engages with Twitter/X finance threads, Whirlpool forums, and Reddit
  • Would run numbers himself before calling anyone
  • Prefers async communication (email, chat) over phone calls
Decision Triggers
  • Reassurance that regional property purchases are within scope
  • A scenario calculator that handles different suburb/property types
  • Transparent eligibility criteria — no hidden "metro only" rules
  • Chat or async support option alongside phone
UX Expectations & CRO Considerations
  • Eligibility page must clarify that regional properties are accepted — this is a frequent drop-off point
  • Add a chat widget for async pre-qualification — this persona won't want to call first
  • The calculator should allow postcode input or property type to build trust that their scenario works
  • Content/blog addressing "using bridging finance for a tree-change" would attract this persona from SEO
  • Reassure that slower secondary market sale timelines are accounted for in loan terms
"We've found our forever home in the regions. We just can't wait forever for Sydney to sell — we need a lender who actually understands how the regional market works."

Critical Gaps

✉️
No async contact option anywhere on site — complete journey blocker P08 explicitly prefers email and may research from regional areas with poor voice connectivity. The complete absence of a mailto: link or email inquiry form on any of the site's pages is a Critical blocker. Every contact path forces a phone call. Findings: F-NAV-04 F-HP-M01 F-BL-M01 F-ELG-M01
📍
Regional property eligibility is opaque The Eligibility page states "some regional areas excluded" without listing which regions. P08 cannot confirm whether his target area (e.g. Orange, NSW) qualifies and abandons rather than calling to find out. Finding: F-ELG-05
🧮
Calculator confusion on mobile The results modal uses jargon ("LVR") without inline explanation and a misleading label ("Refinance existing mortgage") rather than bridging-specific language. On mobile in a regional area with low signal, P08 abandons after encountering unexplained terminology. Findings: F-CALC-M02 F-CALC-M03
🗺️
No regional property scenario examples The calculator and Rates & Fees page use no regional property examples. P08 cannot see himself reflected in the product at any point in the research journey. Finding: F-RF-03

What the Site Does Well

  • Comprehensive FAQ and detail pages (Eligibility, Rates & Fees, Calculator) support deep self-serve research without calling.
  • Eligibility page opening ("Every property owner's situation is unique") signals non-standard scenarios are considered.
  • Site loads quickly and is responsive, supporting research in areas with variable mobile connectivity.

F-NAV-04 — abandonment

P08 arrives hopeful that there's a lender who understands regional relocators. He reviews Eligibility and is concerned to read "some regional areas excluded" — he cannot confirm his region qualifies without calling, which conflicts with his async preference. He moves to the Calculator but encounters blank fields with no regional examples. He calculates his loan amount and sees "LVR 78%" (no tooltip on tap). He reviews the label "Refinance existing mortgage" and pauses — is he supposed to refinance his Sydney mortgage?

He now has three compounding blockers: (1) Cannot confirm his region qualifies, (2) Does not understand the calculator results, (3) Has no way to ask via email. He closes the browser and contacts a competitor lender who offers chat or email contact. Bridgit loses this deal entirely due to a complete contact-path gap. P08 has the highest unmet need on the current site, with an estimated 70–80% abandonment rate before any contact is initiated.

Highest-ROI Quick Win

⚡
Add visible email contact option to Eligibility, Homepage, and Bridging Loan pages F-NAV-04 F-ELG-M01 F-HP-M01 — Expected impact: +40–50% contact initiation rate from async-preferring users; unblocks the J6 journey entirely. One-liner fix: add mailto:hello@bridgit.com.au as a visible contact option alongside the phone number on all key pages. This single fix resolves 4 confirmed findings across 2 audit phases.
